**LinkedIn:**
**Why we chose Bayesian inference over traditional A/B testing**

Traditional frequentist A/B tests answer: "If there were no difference, how surprising is our result?"

This requires:
• Pre-determined sample size
• No peeking at results
• 200–1000 conversions per variant

For an indie founder with 50 sales/month, that's a minimum 6-month wait.

Bayesian tests answer: "Given what we've seen, what's the probability that Price B beats Price A?"

This:
• Updates continuously with each sale
• Works with 20–30 conversions per variant
• Expresses uncertainty as a probability (e.g., "87% confident Price B wins")

The tradeoff is that early estimates have wide uncertainty bands — but that uncertainty narrows quickly. By the time you have 40–60 conversions, you usually have a clear signal.

That's why PricingSim uses Bayesian inference. And why it works for sellers doing $500–$10k MRR.
