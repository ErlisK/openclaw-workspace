## Founder Story / "Why I Built This" (250 words)

I've spent years in the indie creator ecosystem — watching people sell digital products on Etsy and Gumroad with nothing but a "please don't resell this" line in their listing description.

When disputes happen — and they do — those informal notes are worthless. But hiring a lawyer to draft a commercial license is $500+, and most legalzoom-style templates are either too generic, too US-centric, or written in language that confuses both the seller and the buyer.

The real gap isn't legal sophistication. It's friction. Most indie creators skip contracts not because they don't care — they do — but because getting a real one takes too long and costs too much.

PactPack solves this with a 5-question wizard and a library of clause-level templates built for the actual platforms creators use: Etsy, Gumroad, itch.io, and OpenSea. Each contract is jurisdiction-aware (US, EU, UK, AU, CA), exports as PDF/HTML/Markdown, gets a SHA-256 provenance hash for tamper-evidence, and includes a hosted license page buyers can read and accept — with their signature timestamp recorded.

The free tier covers 2 exports/month with no credit card. Unlimited is $9/year — less than a single hour of paralegal time.

I built this because the tools creators actually need have always been priced for Fortune 500 companies. That's wrong. If you sell anything digital, you deserve a real contract.

— The PactPack Team
