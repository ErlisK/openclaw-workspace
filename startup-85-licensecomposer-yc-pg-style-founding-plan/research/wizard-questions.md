# Wizard Questions — LicenseComposer

**Target user:** Digital artist / freelance illustrator taking commissions  
**Goal:** Generate a ready-to-sign Commission Agreement in under 2 minutes  
**Pain mapped:** Scope creep, no deliverable spec, revision abuse, IP confusion, non-payment

---

## The 7-Question Wizard

---

### Q1: What are you creating? *(Work Type)*

**Question:** What type of work are you commissioning / being commissioned for?

**Options:**
- Character illustration / portrait
- Logo or branding
- Concept art (game, film, book)
- Comic pages / sequential art
- Graphic design (social media, merch)
- Other digital art

**Pain addressed:**  
Defines the deliverable category. Without this, "one small change" becomes "redesign the whole thing."  
*Root pain: r/ArtistLounge — "I'm not sure what I'm legally allowed to charge extra for."*

---

### Q2: What exactly will be delivered? *(Deliverable Spec)*

**Question:** Describe what the final deliverable includes:

**Sub-fields (auto-suggested based on Q1):**
- How many pieces / pages / concepts?
- File format(s) (PNG, PSD, SVG, PDF, print-ready…)
- Resolution / dimensions
- Number of characters / elements
- Background: yes / no / simple / detailed
- Color or black-and-white?

**Pain addressed:**  
Locks in scope before work starts. The single most common source of scope creep.  
*Root pain: "My client keeps sending new reference images and saying 'can we incorporate this element too?'"*

---

### Q3: How many revisions are included? *(Revision Policy)*

**Question:** How many rounds of revisions are included in the price?

**Options:**
- 1 revision
- 2 revisions
- 3 revisions
- Custom number: ___
- No revisions (approval-only after sketch)

**Follow-up:** What happens if the client requests more?
- Each additional round: $____ per round
- Not included — original scope only
- At artist's discretion

**Pain addressed:**  
"I agreed to three revisions. They've now asked for nine."  
*Root pain: #3 most-quoted r/ArtistLounge pain. No limit = unlimited free labor.*

---

### Q4: Who owns the final work? *(IP & Copyright)*

**Question:** What rights does the client receive?

**Options (plain English):**
- **Personal use only** — Client can display it for themselves. Cannot sell, print, or use for business.
- **Commercial license** — Client can use the work in a business context (merchandise, advertising, social media brand).
- **Exclusive commercial license** — Artist cannot reuse or resell this design to anyone else.
- **Full copyright transfer** — Artist gives up all rights; client owns the work completely. *(Add-on fee recommended.)*

**Why it matters banner:** "A commission does not include commercial rights unless explicitly stated. The artist retains all rights by default." (shown inline)

**Pain addressed:**  
"The person who ordered the commission has zero rights to sell the work or prints of it unless agreed upon."  
*Root pain: r/ArtistLounge (1ahi4ug) — buyers reselling commissions without rights.*

---

### Q5: What are the payment terms? *(Payment & Deposit)*

**Question:** How will payment be structured?

**Sub-fields:**
- Total price: $____
- Deposit required upfront: ____% (suggested: 50%)
- When is final payment due? 
  - Before file delivery
  - On approval
  - Net-7 after delivery
- Refund policy:
  - Deposit is non-refundable
  - Full refund if cancelled before sketch
  - Partial refund pro-rated by completion stage

**Pain addressed:**  
"Clients ghosting mid-project after I've already put in 20+ hours."  
*Root pain: r/ArtistLounge (8v16i9) — no deposit = no recourse.*

---

### Q6: What platform will this be used on? *(Platform / Jurisdiction context — optional)*

**Question:** Where will this work primarily be used? (Optional — helps tailor license language)

**Options (multi-select):**
- Personal portfolio / display only
- Merchandise / print-on-demand (Redbubble, Printify, etc.)
- Social media (personal brand)
- Game / app / software
- NFT / blockchain
- Commercial advertising / marketing
- Book / publication

**Pain addressed:**  
Platform-specific use cases have different legal implications (game assets vs. merch vs. NFT).  
*Root pain: itch.io forum — "commercial use" means different things on different platforms.*

---

### Q7: Your jurisdiction *(Legal Governing Law)*

**Question:** Which country's laws should govern this agreement?

**Options:**
- United States (+ state selector: default California)
- United Kingdom
- European Union (+ country selector)
- Canada
- Australia
- Other (freeform)

**Note shown:** "Don't see your country? We'll use internationally recognized terms. For country-specific advice, consult a local attorney." *(disclaimer)*

**Pain addressed:**  
"Your business may not be in the EU but your buyers might … as such they are subjected to EU laws."  
*Root pain: itch.io forum (t/1402928) — cross-border licensing creates legal exposure.*

## Output Generated

After the wizard, the user gets:
1. A **plain-English Commission Agreement** (PDF + copy/paste text)
2. A **versioned document ID** (for provenance verification)
3. An **embeddable "Protected by LicenseComposer" badge** for Carrd/Etsy/Gumroad storefronts
4. An optional **email delivery link** to send to the client for acknowledgment
