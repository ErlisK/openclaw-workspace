# Community Analysis — LicenseComposer Research

**Date:** 2026-04-12

---

## 1. r/ArtistLounge

**Threads analyzed:** 8 (hisk4v, tegp98, 9bfepg, cjt1wp, f6vwno, 1ahi4ug, 6iuobw, 8v16i9)

**Key themes:**
- **Scope creep** is the #1 complaint — clients expand work without agreeing on extra pay
- **No contracts** are the norm among new/indie artists; most don't know what to include
- **Revision abuse** — artists agree to 3 revisions verbally, clients demand 10+
- **Non-payment / ghosting** — clients disappear after work starts; no deposit, no recourse
- **IP confusion** — buyers assume a commission includes commercial rights; artists don't explain
- **Template gap** — multiple posts explicitly ask "does anyone have a good commission contract?" showing demand

**Volume signal:** Multiple threads with 100+ comments on commission contract problems. This is an active, chronic pain.

**Quote density:** High — almost every commission thread mentions lack of contract as root cause.

---

## 2. r/gamedev & gamedev.stackexchange.com

**Threads analyzed:** 5 (yz26sw, cj8qzu, 17tbvuf, uq7tbb, stackexchange 137376)

**Key themes:**
- **Asset license ambiguity** — "free for commercial use" is not clearly defined per asset
- **Asset store takedowns** — assets removed after purchase when original creator had no rights; licenses voided
- **Rev-share with no contract** — teams launch games with verbally-agreed splits; disputes arise at payout
- **Work-for-hire ignorance** — studios assume contractor work belongs to them; legally it doesn't without explicit IP assignment
- **Enforcement paralysis** — cost of litigation exceeds potential recovery for small indie titles

**Volume signal:** Legal questions appear in r/gamedev weekly. Lawyer-authored content specifically targeting indie devs is a growing niche.

---

## 3. itch.io Community Forums

**Threads analyzed:** 1 (t/1402928 – commercial use)

**Key themes:**
- Marketplace **refuses to police license clarity** ("ask the creator, we have nothing to do with it")
- Buyers lose trust and **buy elsewhere** when license terms are unclear
- Cross-border **EU/US jurisdiction** creates real confusion for buyers and sellers
- "Commercial use" needs explicit definition — a verbal "yes" from a creator is legally meaningless

**Volume signal:** A single thread captures a systemic problem replicated across every asset marketplace.

---

## 4. Legal Industry Commentary (legalmoveslawfirm.com, argolawyer.com, odinlaw.com)

**Key themes:**
- 90%+ of indie rev-share projects never ship — and even those that do often result in zero payout
- **IP assignment is the most commonly missed clause** in indie game dev agreements
- **Vesting schedules** are almost never included in informal agreements
- Work-for-hire doctrine is routinely misunderstood by both studios and contractors

**Volume signal:** Multiple lawyer-authored resources specifically for indie devs signal commercial demand for simplified legal tools in this space.

---

## 5. NFT / Digital Asset Community (OpenSea, Blur, platform context)

**Key themes:**
- Marketplace **policy changes wiped out creator royalties** (2022–2023 wave)
- Creators had no contractual protection independent of platform behavior
- Buyers misunderstand what an NFT actually conveys (token vs. copyright)
- Smart-contract enforceability is jurisdiction-dependent and poorly understood

**Volume signal:** High noise in 2022–2023; still unresolved. The pain here is **platform dependency**, not just documentation.

---

## 6. Music Producer Beat Licensing

**Key themes:**
- Non-exclusive vs. exclusive licensing is widely misunderstood by artists buying beats
- Producers routinely sell beats without explaining restrictions (stream caps, monetization limits)
- Disputes arise when artists monetize beyond license terms

---

## Cross-Community Patterns

1. **The root cause is always the same:** no written agreement with clear terms
2. **The friction is always the same:** lawyers are too expensive and templates are bad or hard to customize
3. **The damage is always the same:** lost money, broken relationships, or legal liability
4. **The community's solution is always the same:** "you should have had a contract" — but nobody provides one that's actually usable
