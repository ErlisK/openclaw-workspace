# Niche Decision — LicenseComposer

**Date:** 2026-04-12

---

## Decision: Commission Agreements for Indie Digital Creators

**Chosen initial niche:** Digital artists and freelance illustrators who take commissions (character art, logos, concept art, illustrations) and either work without contracts or use inadequate contracts.

---

## Evidence-Based Rationale

### 1. Volume — Highest Quote Density
Category 1 (commission scope creep / unclear deliverables) generated 19 quotes from direct community research — the highest of any category. Multiple threads on r/ArtistLounge exist specifically about commission contract problems, with 50–200 comment counts.

### 2. Frequency — This Pain Is Chronic
Commission-related contract problems appear in r/ArtistLounge, r/ArtBusiness, and r/freelance **weekly**. Unlike NFT licensing (a 2022 spike that's cooling) or rev-share disputes (occasional), commission problems are continuous and growing as more creators monetize their work online.

### 3. Root Cause Is Fixable
The root cause is always: "I had no written agreement" or "the template I found was bad." This is exactly what LicenseComposer solves — it produces a clear, ready-to-use commission agreement in under 2 minutes.

### 4. Clear Demand Signal
- r/ArtistLounge thread *"Does anybody have a commission contract template?"* explicitly asks for exactly what we build
- Community advice always says "get a contract" but nobody provides a good, customizable, platform-aware one
- Existing templates online are generic, US-only, lawyer-written for enterprises, or incomplete

### 5. Low-Friction First Sale
A $9/year subscription to protect commissions worth $50–$5,000+ has an obvious ROI. Unlike rev-share agreements (rare, complex) or NFT licenses (market cooling), commission artists take 5–50+ commissions per year and feel pain regularly.

### 6. Expanding TAM
- Estimated 2M+ digital artists taking commissions (DeviantArt, ArtStation, Twitter/X, Etsy, Gumroad)
- Average commission value: $50–$500
- Even 1% conversion of 100k active commission artists = 1,000 subscribers at $9 = $9,000 ARR as Day 1 floor
- Premium templates (collaboration splits, asset licenses) expand TAM to indie devs and musicians

## Why Commission Agreement Is the Right Beachhead

Paul Graham heuristic: "Make something a small number of people want very much."

- Commission artists **urgently need** a contract every time they take work
- They **know** they need one — community sentiment is overwhelmingly "you should have had a contract"
- They **don't have one** because real lawyer templates are expensive and generic free ones are bad
- The solution is **self-evident** — a 5-question wizard that outputs a ready-to-sign PDF
- The **willingness to pay** is there — the contract protects income worth 10–500x the $9/year subscription
