## Value Curve Narrative

### Archetype A: AI Content Machine
**Peaks at:** Output breadth, ease of use, pricing, brand customization  
**Valleys at:** Evidence depth (1), provenance scoring (1), hallucination mitigation (1), compliance (1), audit trail (1)  
**Strategic problem:** These tools produce content fast and well, but generate hallucinations for any factual/scientific claim. Zero evidence accountability. The science communicator who adopts Jasper learns quickly that it can't be trusted for anything that needs to be true.

### Archetype B: Evidence Research Engine
**Peaks at:** Evidence depth (5), hallucination mitigation (4), provenance scoring (3)  
**Valleys at:** Output breadth (1), ease (3), brand customization (1), compliance (1), audit trail (1)  
**Strategic problem:** Excellent at finding evidence, completely unable to produce content. The researcher who uses Elicit or Consensus still has to manually write the tweet, blog post, or slide copy. The workflow gap is large — evidence search → content production is a multi-hour manual step.

### Archetype C: Citation Manager
**Peaks at:** Citation bundle export (4), pricing accessibility (4)  
**Valleys at:** Evidence depth (3), output breadth (1), compliance (1), provenance scoring (1)  
**Strategic problem:** Citation managers organize references well but don't search for evidence or generate content. They're the end of the workflow (format and export citations) but don't address the start (find and evaluate evidence) or the middle (generate content).

### Archetype D: Social Content Tool
**Peaks at:** Output breadth (4), ease (5), pricing (4)  
**Valleys at:** Evidence depth (1), provenance (1), compliance (1)  
**Strategic problem:** Great for consumer/lifestyle content. Completely wrong for health content. Hootsuite's "compliance" feature is for financial disclosures, not FDA/MLR compliance. Science communicators use Buffer to schedule but have to produce and verify content elsewhere.

### Archetype E: Enterprise MLR/Compliance
**Peaks at:** Compliance (5), audit trail (5), human review (4)  
**Valleys at:** Pricing (1), ease (1), output breadth (2)  
**Strategic problem:** Veeva PromoMats solves compliance but is inaccessible to anyone below $10M revenue. 14-month implementation. Requires dedicated MLR team. The mid-market gap (agencies, health startups, university groups) has no equivalent tool at any accessible price point.

### ClaimCheck Studio: The New Curve
**Score profile:**
- Matches AI Content Machine on: output breadth (4), ease (4), pricing (4)
- Matches Evidence Research Engine on: evidence depth (5), hallucination mitigation (5)
- Matches Citation Manager on: citation bundle (5)
- Approaches Enterprise MLR on: compliance (4), audit trail (5), human review (5)
- **Creates uniquely**: per-claim provenance scoring (5) — no competitor scores above 3

**The new curve does not look like any existing archetype.** It crosses multiple clusters simultaneously, which is the signature of a genuine blue ocean value innovation.

## Focus, Divergence, Compelling Tagline Test

Blue Ocean Strategy requires three criteria for a valid new value curve:

### ✅ Focus
ClaimCheck Studio does NOT try to win on everything. It deliberately:
- Reduces brand customization (2 vs 4 for AI writers)  
- Reduces social scheduling (handoff only vs. full tool)
- Reduces format breadth (7 focused formats vs. 80 generic)

This focus is the source of the cost structure that makes the non-enterprise price point possible.

### ✅ Divergence
The shape of ClaimCheck Studio's value curve is fundamentally different from all five archetypes. It is NOT a repositioned AI writer or a content-enabled literature tool — it is a genuinely different curve shape.

### ✅ Compelling Tagline
> **"The only content studio where every claim earns its citation."**

This tagline passes the Blue Ocean test: it communicates the value innovation (evidence-grounded content) AND implies what the strategy eliminates (unverified AI content), AND is compelling enough to make a target buyer act.
