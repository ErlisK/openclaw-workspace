### Quadrant Analysis

**Q1 (High Evidence, High Ease) — Blue Ocean Zone: ★ ClaimCheck Studio (Target)**
- Currently **empty**. No competitor occupies this space.
- The opportunity: combine best-in-class evidence rigor with publish-ready content outputs.

**Q2 (Low Evidence, High Ease) — The Hallucination Zone**
- Jasper, Copy.ai, Writesonic, Grammarly, ChatGPT, Buffer, Hootsuite
- High content production but zero evidence grounding
- Large market but legally and reputationally risky for health/science content
- Combined ~$500M+ ARR in this zone

**Q3 (Low Evidence, Low Ease) — Legacy Tools**
- Altmetric (tracking only), Zotero/Mendeley (storage only)
- Functional but narrow — researchers use them as components, not end-to-end solutions

**Q4 (High Evidence, Low Ease) — The Research Ghetto**
- Scite, Consensus, Elicit, PubMed, Semantic Scholar
- Excellent evidence rigor but outputs are incomprehensible for non-academic audiences
- Users love the data but can't act on it without significant manual work

**Key Insight:** There is a clear diagonal divide in the market. The richest opportunity — high evidence + high ease — is **completely unoccupied**.

### Quadrant Analysis

**Q1 (High Compliance, High Audience Adaptation) — Regulatory Science Comm: ★ ClaimCheck Studio (Target)**
- **Currently empty.** Veeva/Zinc serve compliance but not audience adaptation.
- The TAM: Every regulated industry that needs to communicate to patients, policymakers, clinicians, and consumers.

**Q2 (Low Compliance, High Adaptation) — Creative Tools Zone**
- Jasper, Copy.ai, Writesonic, Anyword — all excellent at tone/format adaptation
- Zero compliance capability — legal liability for regulated content
- The Conversation: good audience adaptation with strong editorial oversight but manual and not scalable

**Q3 (Low Compliance, Low Adaptation) — Academic Tools Zone**
- Zotero, Mendeley, PubMed, Consensus, Elicit, Scite
- No compliance features, limited audience adaptation
- Designed for academic researchers, not content teams

**Q4 (High Compliance, Low Adaptation) — Enterprise Pharma Tools**
- Veeva PromoMats, Zinc, IQVIA OCE
- Strong MLR compliance but rigid output formats (mostly regulatory documents, not consumer content)
- No plain-language generation, no multi-channel adaptation
- Extremely expensive; 99% of potential users cannot access

**Key Insight:** The entire regulated content market (pharma, biotech, health systems, medical devices) is currently forced to either:
(a) Use Veeva/Zinc (expensive, no audience adaptation), OR
(b) Use creative AI tools (accessible, no compliance)

There is **no viable middle ground** — this is the ClaimCheck Studio opportunity.

### Key Insight

**ClaimCheck Studio's target: upper-right quadrant** — highest pipeline completeness at an accessible mid-market price point. This is the unoccupied zone that enables the blue ocean strategy.
