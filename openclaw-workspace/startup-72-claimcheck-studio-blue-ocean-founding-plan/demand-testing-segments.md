## Phase 2: Initial Segments for Outreach and Validation

**Date:** 2025-05  
**Author:** Founding team  
**Status:** Selected — ready for demand-testing outreach  
**Next action:** Send 30 cold outreach emails per segment; target ≥3 paid pilots per segment within 60 days

---

## Selection Rationale

Three segments were selected for initial demand testing based on four criteria:

1. **Pain acuity** — Does the segment have a burning, documented problem ClaimCheck Studio directly solves?
2. **WTP signal** — Is there budget evidence from user interviews or pricing benchmarks?
3. **Reachability** — Can the segment be reached without enterprise sales cycles (≤30 days to first conversation)?
4. **Compliance forcing function** — Does the segment *need* compliance or evidence features (not just "nice to have")?

Segments that meet all four criteria are Tier 1. Segments meeting 3/4 are Tier 2 (next cohort).

### Segment A: Medical Device & Biotech Marketing Teams

**Definition:** Content and marketing teams at Series A–D biotech companies and Class II/III medical device companies. Typically 2–10 person marketing teams producing scientific content for HCP audiences, investor communications, and patient-facing materials.

**Why this segment first:**
- **Mandatory evidence grounding** — Biotech/medtech marketing content must cite clinical data and published studies. Claims about efficacy, mechanism of action, or clinical outcomes are legally risky without sourced evidence. This isn't a preference — it's a legal requirement (FDA 21 CFR Part 801/807, FTC health claims standards).
- **No adequate tool exists at SMB price** — Veeva PromoMats is overkill and $150k+ for this segment. They currently use: Google Docs + PubMed manual searches + in-house regulatory affairs review cycles (2–5 day turnaround per piece).
- **Time pressure is acute** — Biotech content teams operate at startup velocity (conference in 3 weeks, investor deck due Friday) but under pharma-adjacent compliance constraints. The tension between speed and accuracy is the exact pain ClaimCheck Studio resolves.
- **Budget availability** — Series A+ biotech companies have marketing budgets. $149–499/mo is below the threshold for procurement review at most VC-backed companies. A content manager can expense this without a PO.

**Size:** ~8,000 Series A–D biotech companies in US/EU (Crunchbase); ~3,000 have dedicated content/marketing staff. ~2,000 Class II/III medical device companies with HCP-facing content needs (FDA 510(k) database).

**ICP profile:**
- Title: Head of Marketing, Content Marketing Manager, Scientific Communications Lead, Medical Affairs Writer
- Company: Series A–D biotech OR Class II/III medical device, 20–500 employees
- Current tools: PubMed + Google Docs + Canva + HubSpot; sometimes Veeva or Zinc (but rarely)
- Biggest pain: "I need to produce evidence-backed LinkedIn posts and white papers fast, but every claim needs a citation my regulatory affairs team will approve"

**Demand-testing outreach plan:**
- **Channel:** LinkedIn DM + cold email
- **Target list:** 30 contacts from LinkedIn (search: "content marketing" + "biotech" + "Series B/C/D"), 10 from Slack communities (LifeSciHub, BioTechBay)
- **Hook:** "We're building a tool that turns your manuscript or clinical data summary into cited LinkedIn posts and white papers in 10 minutes. Would you spend 20 minutes telling us if we're solving the right problem?"
- **Target:** 8 discovery calls; 2 paid pilots
- **Qualifying question:** "How many hours per week does your team spend finding and verifying citations for content?" (Expected: 4–10 hours/week)
- **Pricing test:** Offer Agency tier pilot at $499/mo for 60 days, or Professional at $149/mo for solo contributors

**Evidence from interviews:**
- SY-013 (VP Marketing, Series C MedDevice): "We produce 4 white papers and 20 LinkedIn posts per month. Every single one goes through a 3-day regulatory review cycle because nobody trusts our citations."
- SY-008 (Medical Affairs Director, Specialty Pharma): "Our marketing team wants to move fast. My team needs to slow them down to check every claim. ClaimCheck Studio would let us speed up without the risk."

**WTP:** $149–1,500/mo (team of 2–5); $2,000–5,000/mo for teams with active regulatory review workflows

### Segment B: Medical Education (Med-Ed) Agencies

**Definition:** Independent agencies and boutiques (5–50 staff) that produce continuing medical education (CME), medical communications, and scientific publications for pharma/biotech clients. Members of AMWA (American Medical Writers Association) and ISMPP (International Society for Medical Publication Professionals).

**Why this segment first:**
- **Compliance is already their product** — Med-ed agencies are paid by pharma clients specifically to produce compliant, evidence-backed content. Their quality of output IS their reputation. A tool that reduces citation errors and compliance flags directly improves their deliverable quality.
- **Multi-client volume** — A 20-person med-ed agency might produce 50–100 evidence-backed documents per month across 5–10 pharma clients. The volume amplifies the ROI of ClaimCheck Studio vs. individual users.
- **AMWA/ISMPP communities are reachable** — Both associations have active member directories, conferences (AMWA Annual Oct 2025, ISMPP Annual Meeting Jun 2025), and LinkedIn groups. Cold outreach to these members lands with warm context.
- **Mid-market gap pain** — These agencies can't afford Veeva ($150k/yr). They're using a combination of Word + PubMed + Endnote + manual editorial review that creates bottlenecks at citation verification and compliance checking.
- **Agency model scales pricing** — A $499/mo Agency tier becomes an easy ROI justification: if it saves 2 hours/week of a $80/hr medical writer's time, it pays for itself in 3 hours per month.

**Size:** ~3,000 AMWA-registered agencies; ~800 mid-size (5–50 staff) in US/EU that produce pharma-commissioned content. Additionally ~500 CME-accredited providers (ACCME database).

**ICP profile:**
- Title: Managing Director, Senior Medical Writer, Editorial Director, Scientific Director
- Company: Independent med-ed agency, 5–50 staff, producing pharma/device-commissioned content
- Current tools: Word + PubMed + Endnote (or Zotero) + Excel for tracking; sometimes custom SharePoint MLR workflow
- Biggest pain: "Our medical writers spend 30–40% of their time on citation searches and verification. That's our most expensive resource doing the least value-added work."

**Demand-testing outreach plan:**
- **Channel:** AMWA LinkedIn group, ISMPP member directory, cold email to agency principals
- **Target list:** 30 AMWA member agencies (LinkedIn: "medical writing" + "agency" + "pharma"), 10 ISMPP members with agency affiliation
- **Hook:** "We're building a tool that reduces citation research time for medical writers by 60–70%. Would you pilot it on one project and tell us if it works?"
- **Target:** 6 discovery calls; 2 paid pilots (Agency tier $499/mo)
- **Qualifying question:** "What percentage of a medical writer's time is spent on citation finding and verification?" (Expected: 25–40%)
- **Pricing test:** Agency tier $499/mo with unlimited sessions and 20 seats; compare ROI vs. 1 hour of a senior medical writer's time

**Evidence from interviews:**
- SY-003 (MedComms Manager, Global CRO): "300-row Excel spreadsheet as our reference log. If one gets retracted, we have no system to catch it."
- SY-007 (Research Communications Lead): "Our medical writers are PhDs doing PubMed searches. It's like using a scalpel to dig a trench."
- SY-004 (Medical Education Agency Creative Director): "Veeva is a no. We're a 12-person agency. We need something we can actually afford."

**WTP:** $499–5,000/mo depending on agency size and volume; most agencies can justify on per-project cost savings

### Segment C: Academic Research Communications Groups (AI-Avoidant Due to Compliance)

**Definition:** University press offices, research communications departments, and scientific communications staff at R1 research universities, funding bodies (NIH-funded centers, Wellcome Trust, HHMI), and national labs. A specific subsegment: institutions that have issued AI use restrictions or guidelines for research communications due to accuracy/compliance concerns.

**Why this segment first:**
- **The compliance-avoidance problem is the exact pain ClaimCheck Studio solves** — Many academic institutions have banned or heavily restricted ChatGPT/Jasper for research communications because of hallucination risk. They're not looking for "another AI tool" — they're looking for a tool where the AI output is grounded in verifiable evidence. This is ClaimCheck Studio's core pitch.
- **Reputational stakes are high** — A university press office that publishes a science news article with an incorrect claim faces institutional reputational risk. The value of evidence grounding is immediately legible to this audience.
- **Purchase path is accessible** — University comms departments have budget for software tools. $29–149/mo is within a departmental credit card purchase. No procurement review needed at these price points.
- **Non-consumption is high** — Most university comms departments currently produce science news with zero AI assistance (back to manual research + interview-based writing). ClaimCheck Studio doesn't need to displace a competitor — it needs to convert non-consumers.
- **Reference customer value** — A university press office customer ("MIT Communications uses ClaimCheck Studio") is a powerful reference for the med-ed and pharma segments.

**Size:** ~200 R1 research universities in the US (Carnegie classification); ~400 globally. Most have 5–20 person research communications teams. Additionally ~100 major research funding bodies with public communications mandates.

**ICP profile:**
- Title: Research Communications Officer, Science Writer, Director of Research Communications, Press Office Manager
- Institution: R1 research university, national lab, or major research funding body (NIH center, HHMI, Wellcome Trust)
- Current tools: Manual (interview + PubMed search + Word); sometimes Scite or Semantic Scholar for literature context
- Biggest pain: "We can't use ChatGPT because our university has a policy. But writing evidence-backed science stories manually takes 6–8 hours per story. We need a faster, trustworthy way."
- **Key differentiator**: This segment is allergic to "AI writing tool" framing. They respond to "evidence-grounded research communication tool."

**Demand-testing outreach plan:**
- **Channel:** Direct email to university press offices, NASW (National Association of Science Writers) community, LinkedIn
- **Target list:** 30 research communications staff at universities with published AI restriction policies (Berkeley, Columbia, MIT, Stanford, UK Russell Group), 10 NASW members
- **Hook:** "We know your institution has restrictions on AI tools for research communications. ClaimCheck Studio is different — it's not an AI writer, it's an evidence grounding layer. Every output traces back to a PubMed source. Can we show you a 10-minute demo?"
- **Target:** 8 discovery calls; 3 paid pilots (Researcher $29/mo or Professional $149/mo)
- **Qualifying question:** "Has your institution restricted or issued guidance on AI use for research communications?" (Expected: Yes, at most R1 institutions as of 2024)
- **Pricing test:** Researcher tier $29/mo individual; Professional $149/mo for team of 3–5

**Evidence from interviews:**
- SY-006 (University Science Writer): "Our comms office got a memo from the provost: no AI-generated content for science news. But I'm still spending 6 hours writing a 400-word story that should take 90 minutes."
- SY-012 (Research Communications Lead, Funded Research Center): "We want evidence-backed content. We don't want AI writing. Can it do both?"
- SY-002 (Freelance Health/Science Journalist): "Paywall is my biggest problem. I can't open half the papers I need to cite."

**WTP:** $29–149/mo for individual comms staff; $299–999/mo for team/department licenses

## Demand-Testing Execution Plan

### 30-Day Sprint (Segments A, B, C simultaneously)

**Week 1–2: List building + outreach**
- Build 30 contact lists per segment (90 total) using LinkedIn Sales Navigator + AMWA/ISMPP directories + university press office directories
- Draft 3 outreach email templates (one per segment) — differentiated hooks, same 20-min call ask
- Send from `hello@citebundle.com` (branded, not personal Gmail)
- Track in Supabase: `demand_test_contacts` table (name, title, org, segment, email, outreach_date, response_status)

**Week 2–3: Discovery calls**
- Run ≥20 discovery calls total (target 7-8 per segment)
- Use standard 5-question discovery script (see below)
- Record key quotes, pain points, WTP signals
- Store notes in Supabase `demand_test_interviews` table

**Week 3–4: Pilot offers**
- Offer ≥7 contacts a paid pilot (Segment A: Agency $499/mo; Segment B: Agency $499/mo; Segment C: Professional $149/mo)
- Pilot includes: demo walkthrough + 14-day access + weekly check-in call
- Success: ≥3 pilots paying before end of sprint

**Week 4: Segment ranking**
- Score each segment on: conversion rate, WTP, urgency, retention signal, referral potential
- Pick the top 1–2 segments for MVP beta focus
- Update pricing-hypotheses.md with empirical WTP data

### Discovery Call Script (5 questions)

1. **"Walk me through the last time you published a piece of science content. What did the citation/evidence process look like?"**  
   *(Listen for: time spent, tools used, approval steps, frustration points)*

2. **"What would go wrong if a claim in your content turned out to be unsupported or retracted?"**  
   *(Listen for: compliance stakes, reputational stakes, personal accountability)*

3. **"Have you tried using AI tools (ChatGPT, Jasper, etc.) for this workflow? What happened?"**  
   *(Listen for: hallucination experience, compliance blocks, distrust signals)*

4. **"If you could get a first evidence-grounded draft in 10 minutes instead of [X hours], what would you do with that time?"**  
   *(Listen for: volume increase, quality improvement, team leverage signals)*

5. **"If a tool did exactly what I just described — evidence grounding, citation bundle, compliance flags — what would you expect to pay for it per month?"**  
   *(Listen for: WTP anchor, budget owner, approval process)*
