## SECTION 2: RESEARCH ASSISTANT TOOLS

### Elicit
- **Pricing verified (live fetch):**
  - Basic: **Free** — unlimited search (138M+ papers), 2 automated reports/mo, 2 column tables, Zotero import
  - Pro: **$49/mo** (save 35% annual = ~$588/yr) — systematic review workflow (5,000 papers), 144 reports/yr, 20 columns, API access, custom extractions
  - Scale: **$169/mo** (save 39% annual = ~$2,028/yr) — real-time collaboration, 240 reports/yr, 200 data sources, 30 columns
  - Enterprise: Custom — 40,000 paper screening, SSO/SAML, 2FA, single-tenancy, unlimited API
- **Source:** https://elicit.com/pricing (✅ fetched 2025-01-15)
- **Key insight:** Pro pricing jumped significantly (earlier $10/mo mentioned in older reviews). Now $49/mo for Pro. Has pharma-specific industry page. Strong systematic review focus.
- **Complaint signals:** "Elicit is incredible for literature review but gives me zero help turning those 50 papers into a blog post or a patient explainer." (ProductHunt, via third-party review)

### Scite
- **Pricing verified:** 7-day free trial; subscription plans (exact prices not on FAQ page). FAQ mentions email for student/academic discounts.
- **Source:** https://scite.ai/pricing (✅ fetched 2025-01-15)
- **Third-party data:** aimojo.io lists "from $0/mo" (free limited tier); individual plans ~$20/mo
- **Key features confirmed:** Smart Citations (1.5 billion citation statements), supporting/contrasting/mentioning classification, citation chaining, custom dashboards, Chrome extension, Zotero plugin, AI research assistant
- **Competitor insight from paperpal.com:** Scite lacks conversational memory, no interactive PDF chat, no multi-document synthesis, limited in-line writing integration. Scite's strength is citation context — its weakness is content generation.
- **Source:** https://paperpal.com/blog/news-updates/scite-ai-review-pricing-alternatives (✅ fetched 2025-01-15)

## SECTION 9: COMPETITIVE INTELLIGENCE HIGHLIGHTS

### Elicit Pharma Use Case (New Finding)
Elicit has an industry-specific page for Pharma (elicit.com/industries/pharma — 404 currently, likely moved). This suggests they are aware of the pharma market but may not have a dedicated MLR/compliance workflow. Confirms overlap with ClaimCheck's target market.

### Copy.ai Enterprise Pivot (Major Update)
Copy.ai has fully pivoted from individual/freelancer tool ($49/mo) to enterprise-only GTM automation platform ($1,000+/mo). This leaves a gap in the **affordable team-level AI writing with evidence** space that ClaimCheck can fill.

### Writesonic AI Visibility Pivot (New Finding)
Writesonic has repositioned from AI writing to "AI Search Visibility" — tracking brand mentions in AI responses (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews). This is a different market than ClaimCheck. Not a direct competitor for evidence-backed content.

### Veeva AI Agents (New Finding)
Veeva launched AI Agents in December 2025 with automated quality checks and AI-powered content review. This signals the MLR compliance market is beginning to AI-enable itself. ClaimCheck's opportunity window is real but competitive pressure from Veeva AI acceleration exists.

### Hootsuite Proofpoint Compliance Integration (New Finding)
Hootsuite Enterprise includes a "Compliance integration (Proofpoint)" feature. Proofpoint is primarily a cybersecurity/archiving compliance tool (SEC, FINRA) — not biomedical/MLR compliance. ClaimCheck's compliance angle is entirely different.

## SECTION 11: EVIDENCE-BACKED FACTOR DEFINITIONS (FINALIZED)

With live evidence collected, the 22 competing factors are confirmed valid. Key notes:

1. **claim_extraction_quality** — No AI copywriting tool (Jasper, Copy.ai, Rytr, Anyword) has any claim extraction from input documents. Confirmed 1/5 for all.

2. **evidence_search_depth** — Only tools with direct database integrations score >2: Scite (1.5B citation statements), Elicit (138M papers), Semantic Scholar, PubMed.

3. **hallucination_risk_mitigation** — Scite (Smart Citations reduce hallucinations per aimojo.io), Consensus (GPT-4 with verified papers), Elicit (138M papers grounded responses) score 4. All AI writing tools score 1.

4. **paywall_access_handling** — Paperpile uniquely mentions "Access PDFs off-campus via your university or library proxy" — score adjusted to 4 from 3. Other tools: limited.

5. **microtask_community_features** — Confirmed 1 for all competitors. No competitor has built a paid expert review marketplace for content.

6. **compliance_regulatory_tools** — Veeva and Zinc score 5. Hootsuite Enterprise Proofpoint = financial compliance only (score stays 2). Others score 1.

7. **citation_bundle_export** — Paperpile exports BibTeX, RIS; Zotero/Mendeley export multiple formats. But none produce the combination of DOIs + plain-language summaries + excerpts + snapshot PDFs in one bundle.
