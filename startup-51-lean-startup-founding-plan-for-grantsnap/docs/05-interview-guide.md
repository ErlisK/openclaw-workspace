# GrantSnap Discovery Interview Guide
**Version:** 1.0  
**Format:** 30 minutes, Zoom or phone  
**Interviewer:** GrantSnap Research Team  
**Purpose:** JTBD discovery — understand current grant-writing workflows, blockers, tools, and unmet needs

---

## Before the Interview

- **Record consent:** "Do you mind if I take notes? The interview is confidential and all findings will be anonymized."
- **Set expectations:** "This is a research conversation, not a product demo. There are no right or wrong answers — your honest experience is what I'm looking for."
- **Start recording** (if consented)

---

## SECTION 1 — Warm-Up & Context (5 min)

*Goal: Understand who they are and their org's grant activity*

1. Tell me a little about your organization — what you do, who you serve.
2. What's your role there? How long have you been doing this work?
3. About how many grant applications did you submit last year?
4. Is it just you writing grants, or does anyone else help?

---

## SECTION 2 — Last Proposal Deep Dive (10 min)

*Goal: Get concrete behavioral data on the actual workflow — not hypothetical*

> *"I'd like to walk through your most recent grant application — the last one you submitted or are currently working on. Let's go step by step."*

5. What funder was it? What was the grant for?
6. **How did you find out about this grant opportunity?** What triggered you to look for it?
7. Walk me through how you actually assembled the proposal. What happened first?
8. What did you write from scratch vs. what did you pull from previous proposals?
9. **How long did the whole thing take?** From "I'm going to apply" to "submitted."
10. Where did you actually write it — Google Doc? Word? Something else?
11. Did anyone else help? How did you coordinate with them?
12. What was the hardest or most time-consuming part?
13. Did you hit any moments of "I don't know how to answer this"? What did you do?

---

## SECTION 3 — Pattern & Workflow (8 min)

*Goal: Understand systemic patterns, not just one instance*

14. How typical was that proposal compared to others you've written? What usually varies?
15. **Do you reuse language from past proposals?** How? How do you find the right version?
16. Where do you store your past proposals? (Follow up: Is it easy to find what you need?)
17. Have you ever lost proposal language when someone left the org or a hard drive crashed? What happened?
18. How do you track all the grants you're applying to — deadlines, stages, contacts?
19. What does your grant calendar look like? Is there a "grant season"?

---

## SECTION 4 — Discovery & Matching (5 min)

*Goal: Understand how they find grants and assess fit*

20. **How do you find new grant opportunities?** Walk me through your typical search process.
21. About how much time per week do you spend just looking for grants vs. writing them?
22. When you find a potential grant, how do you decide whether to apply? How long does that take?
23. Have you ever spent significant time on a proposal and then realized you weren't a good fit? What happened?
24. What would make finding relevant grants easier?

---

## SECTION 5 — Collaboration & Tools (4 min)

*Goal: Understand team dynamics and tool stack*

25. If a board member or volunteer wanted to help with a proposal, how would that work today?
26. Have you tried involving others and had it go wrong? What happened?
27. What tools have you tried for managing grants? What did you like/dislike?
28. If you had to put a dollar figure on how much time you spend on grant writing per month, what would it be?

---

## SECTION 6 — Problem Prioritization (3 min)

*Goal: Surface latent needs and confirm hypothesis priority*

29. If you could wave a magic wand and fix ONE thing about the grant writing process, what would it be?
30. What would have to be true for you to use a software tool to help with this?
31. Would you pay for that? What would feel like a fair price?

---

## SECTION 7 — Wrap-Up

32. Is there anything about your grant writing experience I haven't asked about that you think is important?
33. Do you know anyone else — another ED, a grant writer, a board chair — who would be good to talk to? Can you introduce me?

*Thank them. Offer to share a summary of findings when research is complete.*
