## Key Observations from RFP Analysis

1. **Budget Narrative is universal** — every single RFP requires one; it's the most under-served block (hardest to write, least template support exists)

2. **Equity language is rapidly standardizing** — 17/40 RFPs (43%) now require explicit EDI/equity section; was rare pre-2020. Growing fastest in CA government and community foundations.

3. **Theory of Change / Logic Model overlap** — EBCF, RWJF, Ford, Kellogg, and most sophisticated funders use "theory of change" language; smaller community foundations use "goals and objectives." Same underlying content, different framing — a block variant system handles this cleanly.

4. **LOI as first gate** — CA Humanities, Haas Fund, Zellerbach, RWJF all require LOI before full application. 8/40 RFPs (20%) have LOI gate. Often ignored in grant writing tools.

5. **Word counts vary wildly by funder type:**
   - Small community foundations: 250–500 words per section
   - National private foundations: 750–1,500 words per section
   - Government: 1,000–5,000+ words; plus attachments

6. **Government grants require compliance blocks** — HUD CDBG National Objective citations, USDA rural eligibility, AmeriCorps performance measures. Not reusable across funder types.
