# GrantSnap — User Interview Synthesis
**Phase 1 Discovery · n=12 Interviews**
**Version:** 1.0 | **Date:** 2025-07-12

---

## Methodology

**Recruitment:** Community foundation program officers, nonprofit LinkedIn groups, local volunteer management Facebook groups, NTEN community, Idealist.org volunteer coordinators.

**Target Profile:**
- Orgs with ≤5 grant-writing staff or volunteers
- Annual budget $0–$500K
- Applied for ≥2 grants in past 12 months
- Not using dedicated grant management software (target: Excel/Google Sheets/email users)

**Interview Format:** 45-min semi-structured Zoom/phone  
**Script focus:** Jobs-to-be-done framework — past behavior, not hypothetical preferences

## Top 5 User Jobs (JTBD Framework)

### JOB 1 — Find the grants that are actually worth applying to
**Frequency:** Universal (12/12)  
**Intensity:** 🔴 High frustration

> *"I spend 3–4 hours a week just on Google trying to find who's funding things like us right now. Half the time the deadline already passed."* — Interviewee 7
>
> *"I have a list of funders from a workshop two years ago. I don't know which ones are still open or if we even qualify anymore."* — Interviewee 3

**Current approach:** Google search, word-of-mouth from other EDs, occasional Candid/FoundationSearch (many can't afford it), email newsletters  
**Key friction:** Information is scattered, stale, or behind paywalls. No "inbox of relevant opportunities."  
**Desired outcome:** Wake up Monday to a shortlist of 5–10 grants they actually qualify for, with deadlines visible

---

### JOB 2 — Reuse and adapt past proposal language without starting from scratch
**Frequency:** 10/12 confirmed this as primary time sink  
**Intensity:** 🔴 High frustration

> *"We rewrite the same mission statement 15 times a year. Every funder wants a slightly different word count or format. I copy-paste from old proposals but half the time I grab the wrong version."* — Interviewee 1
>
> *"Our last grant writer left and took all the 'good' language with her. We had to rebuild from scratch."* — Interviewee 5

**Current approach:** Manual copy-paste from previous proposals, inconsistent folder structures, Dropbox chaos  
**Key friction:** Version control is nonexistent; institutional memory lives in one person's head; adapting blocks for different funders is time-consuming but still faster than rewriting  
**Desired outcome:** A reliable library of pre-written, funder-adaptable narrative blocks they can pull confidently

**✅ Confirms H1 Success Criterion: 10/12 = 83% confirm text reuse (exceeds ≥70% threshold)**

---

### JOB 3 — Know if they qualify *before* investing 20 hours in a proposal
**Frequency:** 9/12  
**Intensity:** 🟠 High

> *"We spent a week on a proposal and then realized their giving priorities had shifted. That felt awful."* — Interviewee 2
>
> *"I want to know in 10 minutes: are we a fit? Not after 3 weeks."* — Interviewee 9

**Current approach:** Reading full RFPs, calling program officers (when accessible), asking peer orgs  
**Key friction:** Eligibility language in RFPs is often vague; "small org" means different things to different funders; no quick "fit score"  
**Desired outcome:** An instant eligibility check against org profile before they decide to apply

---

### JOB 4 — Keep track of deadlines, submissions, and funder relationships without a full CRM
**Frequency:** 8/12  
**Intensity:** 🟡 Medium

> *"We missed a deadline last year. I just forgot. We were applying to 10 things at once."* — Interviewee 10
>
> *"I have a spreadsheet but nobody else updates it. And I'm a volunteer — I can't be the single point of failure."* — Interviewee 6

**Current approach:** Google Sheets, Asana, sticky notes, personal calendars  
**Key friction:** Lightweight tools don't have grant-specific fields (funder relationship, portal login, report due dates); heavy CRMs (Salesforce) too complex for small teams  
**Desired outcome:** Simple grant pipeline view: stage (found → drafting → submitted → reporting), deadline alerts, team visibility

---

### JOB 5 — Bring volunteers/board members into the writing process without chaos
**Frequency:** 7/12  
**Intensity:** 🟡 Medium

> *"Our board chair wants to review proposals but she's not tech-savvy. If I share a Google Doc, someone always edits the wrong version."* — Interviewee 12
>
> *"My volunteers want to help but they're scared they'll break something. I end up doing it all myself."* — Interviewee 8

**Current approach:** Email attachments, Google Docs (shared with "editor" access = chaos), verbal reviews  
**Key friction:** No role-based permissions in free tools; change tracking creates more confusion than clarity  
**Desired outcome:** Simple reviewer/editor roles — volunteers can contribute to assigned sections, can't delete others' work

## Surprises / Non-obvious Findings

1. **LOI-first funders are a blocker** — 4 interviewees mentioned that many funders require a Letter of Inquiry before the full proposal. A tool that only helps with full proposals misses the first gatekeeping step.

2. **"Grant season" seasonality is real** — Several orgs apply heavily in Q4 (Oct–Dec) and Q1 (Jan–Mar). Churn risk is high for summer months unless we create year-round value (reporting, pipeline building).

3. **Relationship > Template** — For larger funders ($50K+), interviewees said funder relationships mattered more than proposal quality. GrantSnap's sweet spot is the $1K–$25K "transactional" grant market.

4. **The contract grant writer persona** — Interviewee 11 (contract writer serving 5–6 small orgs) is a potential power user AND a distribution channel. They'd pay more for multi-org management features.

5. **Fear of looking unprofessional** — Multiple interviewees worried that a template-based proposal would "look like everyone else's." Customization controls matter to credibility perception.

## Implications for Product

| Finding | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Text reuse confirmed (10/12) | Build block library as core MVP, not add-on |
| Discovery is #1 pain | Grant matching must be in MVP, not Phase 2 |
| Eligibility uncertainty | Add simple "fit check" before deep proposal work |
| LOI gap | Include LOI template blocks in initial taxonomy |
| Contract writer persona | Design multi-org workspace from day 1 |
| Seasonality risk | Plan reporting-phase features for off-season retention |
