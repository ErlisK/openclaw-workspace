## Workflow Maps (Common Patterns)

### Community Foundation Workflow (Current State)
```
Applications → Google Form → Manual export to Google Sheets
              ↓
Eligibility check → Manual column in spreadsheet
              ↓
Reviewer assignment → Email with PDF attachment
              ↓
Score collection → Email → Manual entry back to spreadsheet [4-11 hrs]
              ↓
Award decisions → Email to grantees, finance, board
              ↓
Disbursements → Email to finance → Check or bank portal → Manual status update
              ↓
Milestone tracking → Separate spreadsheet tab → Manual reminders [sometimes missed]
              ↓
Outcome reports → Email in varied formats → Manual data extraction [2-6 days]
              ↓
Board report → Manual chart building in Google Sheets/Slides [3-6 hrs/cycle]
```

### GrantPulse Future State
```
Applications → GrantPulse intake form (eligibility screening built-in)
              ↓
Reviewer portal → Scoring with COI check → Auto-aggregated results
              ↓
Award decisions → Generated award letters + applicant portal notifications
              ↓
Disbursements → Milestone gates → Stripe ACH → Auto status tracking
              ↓
Outcome reports → Templated form → Aggregated dashboard
              ↓
Board report → One-click export → Board-ready PDF/web view
```

### Time Savings Estimate (from interviews)
- Score collection/entry: **4–11 hrs/cycle saved** (INT-001, INT-015)
- Disbursement tracking: **2–3 hrs/week during active cycles saved**
- Board report: **3–6 hrs/cycle saved** (INT-001, INT-002)
- Outcome data aggregation: **1–6 days post-cycle saved** (INT-001)
- **Total estimated savings:** 15–40 hrs/cycle → conservatively 30% time reduction

## New Hypotheses Surfaced

**H26:** DAFs have a specific legal compliance need (documented foundation discretion) that current email processes violate.

**H27:** Grant programs serving vulnerable populations (religious, mutual aid, health) require applicant-facing privacy controls — case numbers, restricted visibility.

**H28:** Legitimacy signaling ("looks like a real program") is a purchase driver even for free-tier volunteer-run programs.

**H29:** Speed of disbursement is a program effectiveness problem for microgrant programs serving cash-flow-constrained recipients (small business, mutual aid).

## Segment Prioritization Recommendation (Updated)

1. **Community Foundations (1–3 FTE, 50–150 grants/yr):** Highest density of active buyers, clear WTP, 3–4 week sales cycle. Lead here.

2. **Family Foundations:** Slightly more complex sales (board approval), but Foundant cancellation creates an active market. Strong privacy/compliance hook.

3. **DAF Sponsors (small):** Clear pain (donor visibility, manual reporting). Specific legal compliance concern (H26) adds urgency.

4. **Volunteer-Run:** Large in number, $0 WTP. Free tier customer acquisition — valuable for product validation and word-of-mouth but don't mistake activity for revenue.

5. **Municipal:** Real pain, but 6–18 month procurement cycle. Enter via free tier + arts/neighborhood programs to build trust. Revenue later.
