# GrantPulse Interview Guide v1
## Phase 1 — Customer Discovery
**Version:** 1.0 | **Duration:** 45 minutes | **Format:** Video or phone

---

## Screener Criteria

**Qualify IN if the participant:**
- Works at a community foundation, DAF sponsor, family foundation, or municipal/government grant office
- Is involved in grant program administration (not just grantwriting on the applicant side)
- Organization runs ≥5 grants per year
- They have direct experience with the grant management process (not purely strategic)

**Qualify OUT if:**
- They are a grantee/applicant only
- They work at a large foundation (>10 FTE dedicated to grants, or >500 grants/yr) — not our segment
- They already use an enterprise system AND are satisfied with it

---

## Screener Questions (Pre-Interview)

1. What is your role and organization type? (community foundation / DAF / family foundation / municipal office / other)
2. Roughly how many grants does your organization administer per year?
3. What is your involvement in the grant administration process? (intake, review, disbursement, reporting)
4. What tools do you currently use to manage your grant program? (spreadsheet, email, software tool)

---

## Interview Structure

### Section 1: Warm-Up (5 min)

**Goal:** Build rapport, understand context, confirm segment fit.

- "Tell me about your organization and your role in it."
- "How long have you been running grant programs?"
- "Walk me through a typical grant cycle at a high level — when does it start, how does it end?"
- "How many people are involved in running the grant program on your side?"

*[Note: Listen for team size, grant volume, role clarity, and any frustrations volunteered unprompted.]*

---

### Section 2: Current Workflow Deep Dive (15 min)

**Goal:** Understand the actual end-to-end process, tools used, and where friction lives.

**Application intake:**
- "How do applicants apply? Walk me through what happens when an application comes in."
- "How do you track applications — what system or tool are you using?"
- "What's the most frustrating part of the intake process?"
- "How many hours per week do you spend on grants admin during application season?"

**Review process:**
- "How do you handle the review process? Who reviews, and how do you collect scores?"
- "How do you track which reviewers have scored which applications?"
- "Have you ever had a conflict-of-interest issue with a reviewer? How did you handle it?"
- "What's the hardest part of coordinating your reviewers?"

**Award & disbursements:**
- "Once you make award decisions, what happens next? How do you notify applicants and process payments?"
- "How do you handle milestone-based disbursements, if you do them?"
- "Have you ever had a payment go wrong — sent late, sent twice, sent to the wrong account? What happened?"

**Outcome reporting:**
- "How do grantees report back to you? What format, how often?"
- "What do you do with those reports — how do you store and use the data?"
- "Have you ever tried to aggregate outcomes across all your grantees? What was that like?"

**Board/donor reporting:**
- "How do you report to your board on grant program performance? How long does it take to prepare?"
- *(If DAF)* "How do you give donors visibility into how their funds are being used?"

*[Note: Do NOT mention GrantPulse or any product yet. Let them tell their story.]*

---

### Section 3: Problem Depth (10 min)

**Goal:** Identify the 1–3 most acute problems and quantify severity.

- "Of everything you've described, what's the single biggest pain point in your grant process?"
- "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing, what would it be?"
- "On a scale of 1–10, how severe is [top problem they named]? Why that number?"
- "Have you tried to solve this before? What happened?"
- "What would it mean for you if this problem went away? What would you do with the time?"

**Probes by segment:**

*Community foundations:*
- "How much time do you spend on grants admin vs. grantee support per week?"
- "Has the spreadsheet situation ever caused a mistake that affected a grantee or applicant?"

*DAF sponsors:*
- "How often do donors ask you for an update on their fund? How do you respond?"
- "Is donor privacy a concern with your current setup? How do you protect it?"

*Family foundations:*
- "Who in your organization approves new software or vendor contracts?"
- "Have you looked at tools like Foundant, Submittable, or others? What happened?"

*Municipal offices:*
- "What's your procurement process for new software? What does it require?"
- "Do you have state or federal reporting requirements that affect what data you need to collect?"

---

### Section 4: Solution Reaction (10 min)

**Goal:** Gauge reaction to GrantPulse concepts without pitching. Measure pull.

*"Based on what you've described, I want to share a few ideas and get your honest reaction — not pitching, just exploring."*

**Concept 1: Centralized grant lifecycle tool**
- "Imagine a tool that handled your full grant cycle — intake form, reviewer scoring, award tracking, and disbursements — in one place. What's your first reaction?"
- "What would have to be true for you to actually use something like that?"
- "What would make you NOT use it?"

**Concept 2: One-click board report**
- "If you could push a button and get a ready-to-present board report showing your cycle's results, would that be useful? How much time would that save you?"

**Concept 3: Milestone-gated disbursements**
- "What if disbursements were automatically held until the grantee confirmed milestone completion? Would that solve a problem for you?"

**Concept 4: Donor impact dashboard** *(DAF segment only)*
- "What if donors could log in and see a real-time view of how their fund is deployed — grants made, outcomes reported? Would that change how your donors engage with you?"

*[Note: Watch for energy and specificity. Pull > push. If they start describing use cases unprompted, that's a strong signal.]*

---

### Section 5: Pricing & Procurement (5 min)

**Goal:** Understand budget, WTP, and decision process.

- "Do you currently pay for any grants administration software? What does it cost?"
- "If a tool like this existed and worked well, what's a price range that would make you say 'yes' vs. 'that's too expensive'?"
- "Would you prefer to pay per grant processed, or a flat monthly/annual fee?"
- "Who would need to approve a purchase like this at your organization?"
- "Is there a dollar threshold below which you can approve without board sign-off?"

---

### Closing (2 min)

- "Is there anything about your grant management situation we didn't cover that you think is important?"
- "On a scale of 1–10, how interested would you be in piloting a tool like this if it was free to try?"
- "Would you be open to being looped in as we develop this? We'd love to share early access."
- "Do you know anyone else in grant administration who might have strong opinions about this? We'd love a referral."

## Segment Variants Summary

| Section | CF | DAF | FF | MO |
|---------|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Intake probe | Volume + errors | Donor attribution | Board-driven criteria | Eligibility/compliance |
| Review probe | Volunteer coordination | Advisor alignment | Board member reviewers | Panel diversity |
| Payment probe | Check volume | Timing + fund attribution | Check approval chain | Budget cycles + approval |
| Report probe | Aggregate outcomes | Per-donor reporting | Board presentation | State/federal compliance |
| Pricing probe | Per-grant vs. flat | Bundle with CF platform | Board approval threshold | Procurement + free tier |
