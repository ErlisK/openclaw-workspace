## Key Excerpts

**On the finance/grants disconnect:**
> "I'm in Sage Intacct all day. Our Program Officer is in Excel all day. We speak different languages. Every month I have to reconcile what she thinks has been paid with what I have recorded, and they almost never match perfectly on the first pass. Last month we were off by two grants — one that was approved but not paid, one that was paid to the wrong cycle code."

**On disbursement controls:**
> "Our board established a two-signature requirement for any payment over $5,000. That means every large grant requires two board members to physically sign a check or two approvals in our banking portal. The process is designed for control, which I respect. But it means grants can take 3–4 weeks from award to payment, which grantees hate."

**On audit preparation:**
> "We had an audit last year. The auditor asked me to show the approval chain for every grant over $2,500. I spent 6 days pulling together documentation from emails, Excel, and our bank statements. Everything was there but it took 6 days to find it because it was across three systems. The auditor said 'you should have a grant management system.' I said 'I know.'"

**On outcome tracking:**
> "From a finance perspective, I want to know that every dollar was spent on what it was supposed to be spent on. We get final reports but they're narrative. There's nothing that links the financial reporting to the programmatic reporting. If a grantee spent money differently than approved, I'd have to compare their invoice to their narrative by hand. That has happened twice."

**On WTP:**
> "If something integrated with Sage Intacct and gave me the audit trail I need, I could probably justify $300–500 a month. That's real money but the audit prep cost me 6 days. If I can get that to half a day, the ROI is obvious."
