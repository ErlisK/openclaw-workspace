## Key Excerpts

**On running it without staff:**
> "We're a board-run foundation. All four of us take turns doing admin. The problem is that means different things to different board members. My brother uses a different naming convention for files than I do. My sister-in-law accidentally deleted our 2022 award letters folder. Nobody knows who's supposed to follow up with grantees."

**On board meeting prep:**
> "Our board meets for brunch four times a year. We talk about funding priorities and usually by dessert we've made all the decisions. The next day someone has to turn that conversation into a formal record, send award letters, and initiate payments. That someone is usually me because I organized the brunch and took notes."

**On compliance:**
> "Our legal counsel mentioned last year that our grant documentation is 'minimally compliant.' That worried me. We keep everything in a Google Drive but there's no formal approval chain documented. It's emails between family members. If there was ever a dispute — with a grantee, with the IRS, whatever — I'm not sure we could defend our process."

**On pricing:**
> "For a board-run foundation at our scale, under $1,500 a year feels right. We'd want to try it for a full cycle before committing to anything annual."
