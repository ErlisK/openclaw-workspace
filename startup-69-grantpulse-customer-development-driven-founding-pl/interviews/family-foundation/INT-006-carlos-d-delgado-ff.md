## Key Excerpts

**On the current setup:**
> "I'm the only employee. The board is all family members who are passionate about the mission but not interested in the administrative side. I have a board meeting every two months. I spend the two weeks before each meeting in a state of moderate panic because the board report is never ready until the night before."

**On previous Foundant use:**
> "We were on Foundant for three years. We cancelled last year. It was $800 a month and we used about 20% of the features. The part we actually needed — simple online applications, reviewer scoring, basic disbursement tracking — was buried under all this complexity designed for large foundations. I spent more time maintaining Foundant than I saved using it."

**On current Excel system:**
> "I built my own system in Excel. It works because I built it and I understand it. But it's entirely dependent on me. If I left — and I'm not planning to, but — there would be no usable record of anything. The entire grant history is in my head and this spreadsheet."

**On compliance and audit:**
> "Our family patriarch is a former IRS attorney. He cares deeply about documentation. Every grant needs to be defensible — proof that we followed our criteria, that the reviewer process was sound, that we can show the approval chain. In our email-and-Excel system, that documentation exists somewhere in my Outlook inbox. Which is not ideal."

**On donor privacy:**
> "One of our board members is very private about their philanthropic activities. They do not want grant recipients to know which family member championed their application or even that we're a family foundation in some cases. Our current system has no concept of role-based privacy. It's one spreadsheet everyone with access can see."

**On the board report:**
> "I've gotten faster at building it over the years. It's still 3 hours every board meeting. But what I hate is that each board member wants something slightly different. One wants a pipeline view, one wants outcomes, one wants financials. I've never found a way to produce all three without building three different sections of the same document."

**On pricing:**
> "Under $2,500 a year I could approve it myself. Over that I need board discussion. They've been burned by software before — we paid Foundant for something we didn't fully use. So the pitch to them would need to be 'this is specifically what we need, it doesn't do anything else, and we've tried it.'"
