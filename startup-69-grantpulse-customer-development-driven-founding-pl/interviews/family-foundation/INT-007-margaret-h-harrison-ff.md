## Key Excerpts

**On the volunteer-run reality:**
> "I do this because I love the foundation and the family asked me to. I'm not paid. I put in about 15 hours a week during grant season. The rest of the year maybe 5. What I want is something that makes those 15 hours feel worthwhile, not like I'm fighting with a spreadsheet."

**On payments:**
> "We pay grantees from a personal check that the family patriarch writes. I send him a list, he writes checks, they get mailed. Sometimes they get lost. Sometimes he forgets. Last year one check sat on his desk for three weeks and the grantee thought we weren't going to pay them."

**On reporting:**
> "We don't really require formal outcome reports. We ask grantees to 'check in with us' in 6 months. Some send emails, some call, some do nothing. One of our board members is trying to push for more formal accountability but we don't have a system for it."

**On the family board dynamics:**
> "Our board meets over dinner four times a year. The whole meeting is conversation, then at the end someone says 'okay what did we decide?' I take notes. That is our grant decision process. I try to write a summary afterwards but there's no structured record."

**On pricing:**
> "Free would be ideal. We have no software budget. I'd advocate for paying something if it really made my life easier — maybe $50-$100 a month. But I'd need to convince the patriarch and he's skeptical of anything that costs money for 'just software.'"
