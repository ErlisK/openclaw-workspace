## Key Excerpts

**On the IT/procurement barrier:**
> "Any new software requires a security review from our city IT department. That takes 6–12 weeks minimum. Then it goes to procurement. Then it comes back for final approval from my department director. We've been trying to get approval for a document management system for 8 months. It's still in procurement."

**On CDBG compliance reporting:**
> "CDBG requires very specific data fields in the annual report — beneficiary demographics, income levels, activity categories. My Excel tracker has never matched exactly what HUD asks for. Every year I spend about two weeks in August reformatting my data to fit the reporting template. It's the same data, just in a different shape."

**On disbursements:**
> "Our payment process is: I fill out a check request form, submit to finance, they process it in their system, and the check is mailed. The fastest it has ever happened is 10 business days. Usually 3–4 weeks. I have grantees who need to pay contractors before the check arrives and they have to front the money, which some of them can't afford."

**On reviewer coordination:**
> "Our review committee is a mix of city staff and community members. Community members can't access SharePoint. So I print applications, drive them to people's homes or leave them at city hall, collect their scoring sheets, drive back, scan them, enter them. It's 2024 and I'm physically transporting paper applications."

**On the city council accountability pressure:**
> "I present to city council twice a year. They want to see the impact of CDBG investment — jobs created, units of housing, people served. I can tell them what was funded. I often can't aggregate the outcomes because my grantees report in their own formats. I had a council member say 'this report is just a list of who got money.' He wasn't wrong."

**On what would make a tool work in this environment:**
> "It would need to be free, or cheap enough that I can call it a program expense and hide it in my budget. It would need to work on Chrome — that's all our city computers have. It would need to be something I can describe to IT as 'it's just a web form' so it doesn't trigger a full security review. And it would need to export in the formats HUD accepts."

**On pilot interest:**
> "If it's free and you're not asking me to put any city data in it for the pilot, I could probably try it for our arts grant program, which is separate from CDBG and not subject to the same federal restrictions. That's 12 grants a year. Low stakes. If it works, I build the case to use it for CDBG."
