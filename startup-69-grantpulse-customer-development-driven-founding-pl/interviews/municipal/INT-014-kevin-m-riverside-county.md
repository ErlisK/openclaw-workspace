## Key Excerpts

**On the AS400 problem:**
> "Our county financial system is from the 1980s. It runs on a green-screen terminal. I am not joking. Every grant payment request I submit gets processed through a system that predates the internet. When I tell this to people at conferences, they always think I'm exaggerating. I'm not."

**On grant reporting:**
> "We're required by the state to report outcome data on all county-funded health grants. The state wants specific public health metrics — vaccination rates, screening numbers, ER visit reductions. My grantees report these in their own way. I spend 3 weeks every year translating their reports into the state format."

**On what keeps him on the current system:**
> "County IT has a list of approved software. If it's not on the list, I can't use it. The process to get something added to the list takes 6–18 months. I don't have that kind of time for any individual problem. So I work around it. I've been working around it for 4 years."

**On the pilot question:**
> "I'd have to use it completely outside county systems. I could run my next cycle on paper and simultaneously enter things into your system, just to test it. But nothing county-related could actually run on it without IT approval. Which takes... you already know the answer."

## Debrief Notes

**County IT is even more restrictive than city.** AS400 legacy system is an extreme case but signals the depth of technical lock-in in government contexts.

**This interviewee is NOT a near-term pilot candidate.** But his referral to colleagues at community foundations is valuable.

**R03 CONFIRMED IN SPADES:** County procurement timeline = 6–18 months minimum. Don't prioritize this sub-segment early.

**Key insight:** State-level health reporting requirements create a distinct compliance format need beyond CDBG. If GrantPulse ever pursues municipal, will need configurable export formats.
