## Key Excerpts

**On the speed pressure:**
> "Small business owners who apply for our microgrants are not patient. They're dealing with cash flow. A three-week payment cycle is a problem. I've had two businesses tell me they would have used the grant for something time-sensitive but it came too late and they found another way. What's the point of the program if the money arrives after the need?"

**On the review process:**
> "I have a review committee of four city staff members. We all use city email. When I send applications for review, they review them during lunch breaks or evenings because it's not really their job. Getting scored rubrics back takes forever. Last cycle it took 19 days."

**On outcome reporting:**
> "We ask grantees for a 6-month check-in. About 60% respond. The ones who do send me an email in their own format — usually something like 'hey, we used the money for X, here's how it helped.' That's it. I can't aggregate that into anything useful for my department director."

**On the city council reporting requirement:**
> "Every year the mayor does a 'state of economic development' report. I'm asked to contribute a section on our microgrant program. I show the number of businesses funded, total dollars, geography. What I can't show — and what I've been asked to show — is job retention or job creation. Because I don't have a consistent way to collect that."

**On the pilot:**
> "I can move fast. I have a grant cycle starting in 60 days. If you can get me something I can use without going through IT — like, is it just a website? — I'll try it."
