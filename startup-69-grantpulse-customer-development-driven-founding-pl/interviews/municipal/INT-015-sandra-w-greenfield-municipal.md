## Key Excerpts

**On being "lucky" with Google Workspace:**
> "We're unusual — our city approved Google Workspace a few years ago. So I can use Google Forms and Google Sheets. That's better than some of my colleagues who are on SharePoint and can't use anything Google. But Google Forms is not a grant management system. It's a questionnaire. I'm still doing everything manually after the form."

**On PayPal:**
> "For our small grants under $1,000 we actually pay via PayPal. It's fast, grantees like it. But our finance department hates it because PayPal fees come out of the grant amount, so grantees get $970 instead of $1,000. Finance has been trying to get us to stop. But what's the alternative? The regular check process takes 4 weeks."

**On the biggest win a tool could give her:**
> "If I could stop spending two weeks after every cycle manually entering scores from my email into a summary spreadsheet, that alone would make me say yes to a tool. I can describe the problem in exact hours: 11 hours per cycle × 4 cycles per year = 44 hours a year I spend on score entry. That's a full week of my life, every year, just entering data that should flow automatically."

**On the pilot:**
> "Our next cycle is in 8 weeks. I've been asking my director to find something better for 2 years. If you can have something ready in 6 weeks that I can use for the next cycle, I will be your biggest advocate. I don't need it to be perfect. I need it to be better than what I'm doing."
