## Key Excerpts

**On the current system:**
> "I am the system. I know where everything is because it's all in my head and in my email. That's terrifying. I've been doing this for 12 years and if I left tomorrow, no one would know how to run the program."

**On reviewer coordination:**
> "We have six community reviewers. Four of them are over 65, not particularly tech-savvy. I print the applications and mail them — actual physical mail. Then they call me with their scores or mail them back on a scoring sheet I designed in Word. I then enter all of it into my spreadsheet. It takes me an entire weekend every cycle."

**On payments:**
> "We write checks. My bookkeeper, who comes in 10 hours a week, writes the checks. She works from a list I give her. Once she wrote a check for $1,000 instead of $10,000 — just a transcription error — and the grantee didn't notice for two weeks because they were waiting for the deposit to show up. When it did, it was the wrong amount, they called us, we felt terrible."

**On reporting:**
> "My board is old school. They want a paper report. I print a summary every board meeting. I type it from scratch every time using my spreadsheet as the source. I don't have a template. It takes me probably four hours."

**On switching to software:**
> "My concern is always: what happens if the company goes away? We had a donor database software vendor go bankrupt two years ago and we lost three years of data. I will never trust a small company with my data again unless they can guarantee I can export everything."

**On pricing:**
> "I look at everything through the lens of: is this justifiable to the board? Under $1,500 a year I don't even need to tell them. $1,500 to $5,000 I mention it. Over $5,000 it goes to the governance committee. For software for just me, I wouldn't want to go over $2,000."
