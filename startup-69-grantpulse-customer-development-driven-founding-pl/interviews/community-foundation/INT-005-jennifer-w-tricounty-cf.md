## Key Excerpts

**On Foundant GLM:**
> "We pay about $7,200 a year for Foundant. It does most of what we need. But it's complicated. We have two staff who are the only ones who really understand how to configure it, and when one of them left last year, we had a nightmare cycle where reports weren't set up right and we couldn't get data out in the format the board wanted."

**On what Foundant doesn't solve:**
> "The disbursement side is still manual. Foundant tracks that a payment is due, but it doesn't actually send money. Our finance team is in QuickBooks and they operate completely separately. I spend probably 3 hours a week during active cycles just reconciling between Foundant and what finance actually did."

**On outcome reporting:**
> "Grantees submit outcomes in Foundant, which is good. But our outcome questions vary by program. So I have five different report forms and five different data structures and I still can't produce a cross-program impact summary without manually building a spreadsheet."

**On small program vs. large program pain:**
> "We're probably too big for your target. But I have colleagues at smaller foundations — under 50 grants a year — who are still on spreadsheets because Foundant's minimum commitment is too high for them. If you can serve that segment below us, there's definitely a market."

**On pricing signal:**
> "We'd probably stay on Foundant unless you had something truly better. But the $7,200 a year feels high for what we actually use. If someone offered us 80% of Foundant's functionality for $3,000 a year, we'd take that meeting."
