## Transcript (Condensed)

**INTERVIEWER:** You mentioned you're using Airtable — how's that going?

**DAVID:** It's better than it was when we were on Excel. But we've basically rebuilt an Excel spreadsheet in Airtable, just with a nicer interface. We don't use most of what Airtable can do. We use it as a database. Someone paid $15,000 to an Airtable consultant to set it up two years ago and now I'm the only one who knows how it works.

**INTERVIEWER:** What's the gap Airtable doesn't fill?

**DAVID:** The reviewers. They can't use Airtable — the license cost would be insane for 8 people who only log in twice a year. So we still do the review process outside the system. We create a custom Google Form for scoring each cycle, import the results back into Airtable, then cross-reference to make sure nothing was missed. It's a lot of manual glue work.

Also the payment side. Airtable knows who's been awarded but it has no connection to anything financial. My finance colleague works in QuickBooks. She has no idea what's happening in Airtable unless I tell her. Every disbursement is me emailing her "please pay X org $Y by Z date." She creates the check or ACH, I update Airtable manually. It's two systems that don't talk.

**INTERVIEWER:** Walk me through a typical pain moment in the process.

**DAVID:** The worst is when we're trying to do milestone-based grants. We have a few larger grants — $5,000 to $15,000 range — where we pay in two tranches: 60% upfront, 40% when they submit a progress report. In theory. In practice, I have a spreadsheet of who's supposed to submit what by when, and I email reminders manually, and about 30% of grantees are late, and sometimes my finance colleague releases the second tranche before I check my spreadsheet. We've over-paid twice. Both times we had to ask for money back. That's not a conversation anyone wants to have.

**INTERVIEWER:** How do you produce your board report?

**DAVID:** Our board wants a one-page "state of grantmaking" and a 12-slide deck every quarter. I export from Airtable, manipulate in Google Sheets, build charts, copy to slides. Every time I do this — which is four times a year — I think "why is this so manual?" We spent $15,000 on Airtable setup and this is still a 6-hour job every quarter.

**INTERVIEWER:** What would you rate the overall admin pain level?

**DAVID:** Eight out of ten. We have a better situation than orgs on spreadsheets, but we still have all the same underlying problems — reviewer coordination, disbursement tracking, report aggregation. Airtable added a layer of complexity without solving the fundamental issues.

**INTERVIEWER:** What would you want from an ideal tool?

**DAVID:** I want one thing that does everything. Intake, scoring, awards, disbursements — with actual gates so my finance person can't accidentally pay someone early — and reporting. And I want it to have a dashboard I can show the board without building 12 slides.

**INTERVIEWER:** Pricing range you could work with?

**DAVID:** We already pay $4,200/year for Airtable and it doesn't do what I need. If something replaced Airtable and filled all the gaps, I could make a case for that budget. Maybe a bit more — $5,000/year feels like the ceiling my ED would approve without a board discussion.
