# Interview Transcript — INT-024
## Community Foundation — Program Director, Switching From Submittable

**Date:** 2025-07-17  
**Duration:** 48 minutes  
**Format:** Zoom  
**Participant:** Nathaniel B., Director of Community Investment  
**Org:** Brookside Community Foundation  
**Segment:** Community Foundation  
**Grant Volume:** 110 grants/year  
**Tools:** Submittable (evaluating cancellation), Excel for post-award  
**Problem Severity:** 8/10  
**Pilot Interest:** 9/10 — actively looking for Submittable replacement  

---

## Key Excerpts

**On why they might leave Submittable:**
> "Submittable is good for what it does. Applicants love the portal. Our reviewers find it intuitive. The problem is it stops at the award letter. The moment we click 'award,' everything moves to Excel. I'm paying $11,000 a year for a really nice application intake form."

**On double-entry:**
> "Every awarded applicant gets entered into our post-award tracking spreadsheet manually. Name, amount, payment schedule, milestone dates, report due dates. It takes me or my assistant about 4 hours at the end of each cycle to do this data transfer. That's time we pay for and it's pure overhead."

**On the milestone problem:**
> "We have 30 grants this cycle with milestone-based disbursements. I manage this from a spreadsheet that I review every Monday morning. I email reminders manually. Sometimes I'm late sending reminders. Sometimes grantees are late submitting reports and I don't catch it until the next week's review. Last cycle we paid a second tranche to a grantee who hadn't filed their interim report. When we caught it, we asked for the money back. They had already spent it."

**On what he's actively looking for:**
> "I am actively evaluating Submittable alternatives. I need: intake that's as good as Submittable, post-award milestone tracking with automated reminders, Stripe or ACH integration for disbursements, outcome report collection, and board-ready reporting. If something does all that for less than $11,000 a year, I'm switching."

**On timeline:**
> "Our Submittable contract renews in 4 months. I need to make a decision in 3 months or we're locked in for another year. If you can show me a working product in 6 weeks, I'll evaluate it seriously."
