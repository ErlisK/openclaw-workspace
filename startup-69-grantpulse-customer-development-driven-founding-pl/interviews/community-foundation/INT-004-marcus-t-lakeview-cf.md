## Key Excerpts

**On cancelling Submittable:**
> "We used Submittable for two years. The intake was great. Applicants loved it. The review interface was fine. But once you made an award, Submittable basically stopped. There was no disbursement tracking, no milestone management, no way to track grantee reports coming in. So we were still doing all of that in Excel, which meant we had two systems and double entry. We cancelled it. Couldn't justify $900 a month for a better intake form."

**On current state:**
> "We went backwards. We're on Google Forms now. Which is free. And terrible."

**On the specific gap:**
> "What I needed from Submittable was everything after the award letter. That's the complicated part — that's where things fall apart. The intake is actually the easy bit. Any Google Form can collect applications. What's hard is: once someone is funded, how do you track what they owe you, when they're supposed to deliver it, and whether you've paid them the right amount at the right time? That's what nobody does well."

**On board reporting:**
> "Our board chair is a former banker. She wants the impact numbers every quarter. I build a report from scratch every time. She asked me once if I could 'just export it from the system.' I laughed. In a sad way."

**On pricing:**
> "If something did everything Submittable did plus post-award — milestone tracking, disbursements, outcome reports — I'd pay $200 a month. Easily. That's less than Submittable and it does more."
