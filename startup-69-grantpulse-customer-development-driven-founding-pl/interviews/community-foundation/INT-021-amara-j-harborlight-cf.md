## Key Excerpts

**On inheriting the system:**
> "I came into this role six months ago. My predecessor left no documentation. What she left was a Google Drive with 47 folders, a master spreadsheet that's password-protected — and she's not answering my calls — and an Outlook inbox that was forwarded to me but I can't find anything in because she didn't use folders. I've been rebuilding from scratch and I'm five months in and I'm still finding things we owe grantees that nobody told me about."

**On the specific incident:**
> "I found out two weeks ago that we missed a final report deadline to a pass-through funder. The grant was in the spreadsheet but the report deadline wasn't — my predecessor apparently kept that in her head. We may lose $45,000 in future funding because of a deadline that existed in exactly one place: her memory."

**On what she needs most:**
> "I need a system that any competent professional can pick up and understand. Not just me. Not a spreadsheet built by one person. A real system with real documentation. I don't want my institutional knowledge to be as lost as hers was."

**On the urgency:**
> "I'm going through my first grant cycle without any prior-year knowledge to guide me. Every decision I'm making is a guess or a deduction. I need to document everything as I go. There's no tool helping me do that."

**On pricing:**
> "I have discretionary authority up to $3,000/year. The ED would support me going to the board for more if I had a strong case. After what happened with the missed deadline, the board is sympathetic to 'we need better systems.'"
