## Key Excerpts

**On speed as the primary need:**
> "We're a mutual aid fund. Speed is everything. People apply because they have an emergency — rent due tomorrow, utility shutoff notice. We try to approve within 24 hours and pay within 48. Our current system does this okay, but it's chaotic. I'm manually checking applications, texting my co-coordinators, and then sending Zelle payments from my personal phone. At 11pm sometimes."

**On the personal payment problem:**
> "I paid out $8,000 in Zelle transfers last year from my personal bank account. I'm then reimbursed from our fund account. This is a compliance nightmare. I've been told by someone with accounting knowledge that this creates personal tax liability for me. I'm still doing it because it's the only way to meet our speed requirement."

**On volunteer coordination:**
> "We have 5 volunteers. Reaching consensus on an application happens over a WhatsApp group. Messages get lost, people are in different time zones, sometimes an application sits for a day because no one responded to the thread. I want a place where reviewers can see applications and vote, not a WhatsApp chain."

**On what a tool would need:**
> "Free — obviously. Fast. And it would need to either integrate with payment apps people already have or have its own payment system. I can't give up speed for compliance. If the tool is compliant but slow, it doesn't work for us."
