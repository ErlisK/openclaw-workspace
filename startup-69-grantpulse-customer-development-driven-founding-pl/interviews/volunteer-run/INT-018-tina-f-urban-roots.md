# Interview Transcript — INT-018
## Volunteer-Run Program — Community Garden Small Grants

**Date:** 2025-07-10  
**Duration:** 25 minutes  
**Format:** Phone  
**Participant:** Tina F., Volunteer Grant Coordinator  
**Org:** Urban Roots Community Garden Network  
**Segment:** Volunteer-Run  
**Grant Volume:** 15 grants/year ($300–$1,500)  
**Budget:** $9,000/year  
**Team:** 2 volunteers  
**Tools:** Google Forms, email, personal checks, Instagram  
**Problem Severity:** 6/10  
**Pilot Interest:** 9/10  

---

## Key Excerpts

**On running a grant program as a side project:**
> "This is maybe 5 hours of my week during application season, maybe 1 hour the rest of the time. It's a side project. But it matters — those 15 grants go to community gardens that grow food for people who need it. I want it to be professional even if I'm doing it in my spare time."

**On the current pain:**
> "I lose track of where applications are. Did I email that person back? Did I send their check? I had a grantee follow up twice asking about their payment because I forgot to tell my co-coordinator to send the check. I felt horrible."

**On what they'd switch for:**
> "Something that sends automatic status emails to applicants. That one thing would save me 2 hours a week and prevent me from lying awake wondering if I forgot to reply to someone."

**On pricing:**
> "Free is the only option. But if this thing works and I'm still using it in a year, I'd ask our network board if we could contribute $20–30 a month. Like a donation to a software tool that helps us."

## Debrief Notes

**Key insight: Applicant communication automation** is the single biggest value prop for very small volunteer programs. Not the full lifecycle — just "did I reply to everyone?"

**WTP:** $0 now → possible $20–30/month after value proven. This is the freemium conversion path.

**The "lying awake" quote** is a strong emotional signal about the stakes these volunteers feel.
