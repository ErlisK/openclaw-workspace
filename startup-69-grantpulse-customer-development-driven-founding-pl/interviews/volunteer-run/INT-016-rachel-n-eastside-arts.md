## Key Excerpts

**On running a grant program with zero budget:**
> "We started this program because artists in our neighborhood needed small amounts of money fast — $200 for supplies, $500 for a performance. We're all volunteers. We have no overhead budget. We raised $18,000 last year from individual donors and we gave it all out as grants. Every dollar we spend on software is a dollar that doesn't go to an artist."

**On the current process:**
> "Google Form for applications, Google Sheet for tracking, I personally review all applications with my two co-founders over a Zoom call on Sunday afternoons. We Venmo the money. That's the whole system."

**On what breaks:**
> "What breaks is the Venmo part. Venmo has limits. We've hit them. And some grantees don't have Venmo. So we write personal checks from a co-founder's account. It's technically correct but it looks terrible — a personal check from a private individual. One grantee asked if we were a real organization. That hurt."

**On outcome tracking:**
> "We ask grantees to post on Instagram and tag us. That's our outcome reporting. Some do, most don't. Our donors — who are individual people we know — love seeing the Instagram posts. But there's no formal record of what the grants accomplished."

**On what they need:**
> "Free, simple, and trustworthy. We need to look like a legitimate grant program even though we're all volunteers. An application form that doesn't look like Google Forms, a review process that doesn't happen over Zoom with no record, and a way to pay grantees that isn't Venmo. That's it."

**On freemium:**
> "If it was free for our size, I'd switch tomorrow. We'd tell every other small grant program we know about it. We're connected to probably 15 other volunteer-run programs in our city."

## Debrief Notes
