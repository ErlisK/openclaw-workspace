## Key Excerpts

**On the DAF donor visibility problem:**
> "Our donors range from people who want to be completely hands-off — they give us money and trust us — to people who call every week asking what's happening with their fund. The hands-off donors are easy. The engaged donors are a lot of work. For every fund I manage, if that donor calls, I have to manually pull up their history, check their balance, look at what grants were made, and then verbally summarize it. There's no portal they can log into."

**On a specific donor loss:**
> "We lost a $200,000 fund six months ago. The donor moved it to Schwab Charitable. When I asked them why, they said 'Schwab has a mobile app and I can see everything in real time. You send me a PDF in January.' That's... hard to argue with."

**On the reporting burden:**
> "For our more engaged donors, I produce a quarterly 'fund summary' — one per donor. I have 38 DAF donors. If even half of them are 'engaged,' that's 19 custom PDFs every quarter. I do them all in Word, manually. It takes me about 2 days per quarter just to produce donor reports. That's 8 days a year I spend on something that should be automated."

**On grant consistency across fund advisors:**
> "Each of our DAF donors recommends grants from their fund. Some are very sophisticated — they know how to structure a recommendation, they know the eligibility rules. Others recommend grants that we can't make — wrong org type, wrong geography — and we have to go back and explain why. There's no intake form to filter that. I just get emails."

**On compliance:**
> "DAF grants legally have to be made at our discretion, not the donor's. That's fundamental to DAF structure. But some of our donors act like it's their money to direct freely. We need better documentation that shows our board made each grant decision based on our criteria, not just based on what the donor said. If we were ever audited on this, the current system would not look good."

**On pricing:**
> "Our foundation's tech budget is not large. I'd have to make a case to our CFO. If something reduced donor calls by 50% — which is maybe 3 hours a week — and automated those quarterly reports — another 2 days a year — I could make that case easily. I think we could do $400–600 a month for the right thing."
