## Key Excerpts

**On donor visibility:**
> "We're a small program. Our 18 donors are mostly local business owners and retirees. Most of them are fine with the annual letter. But I have three donors who are really active — they want to know what's happening, they forward me names of organizations they want funded, they want to see impact. I can't serve them well because I don't have the tools."

**On impact reporting:**
> "One of our top donors gave us $75,000 in the last three years. She's asked me twice for an impact summary of her fund — not just the grants made, but 'what did those grants accomplish?' I can tell her what organizations we funded. I cannot tell her what those organizations did with the money because our grantee reports are all over the place."

**On operational load:**
> "I spend about a quarter of my time on the administrative side of our DAF program. That's probably 10 hours a week. For an 18-fund program, that seems excessive. Most of it is email — explaining our process, following up on recommendations, sending manually built reports."

**On pricing:**
> "For a program our size, I'm thinking $200–300 a month would be the conversation I'd need to have with my ED. She's financially conservative. But if it reduced my admin hours by half and we could retain that one donor who's asking hard questions — I think I could make that case."
