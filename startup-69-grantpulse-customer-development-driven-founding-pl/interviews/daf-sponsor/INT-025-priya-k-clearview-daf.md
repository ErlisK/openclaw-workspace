## Key Excerpts

**On being early:**
> "We launched our DAF program six months ago. We have 7 donor accounts. I know what's coming — I've talked to colleagues at other foundations who've been doing this for 10 years and they're drowning in manual work. I want to build the right system from the beginning instead of spending years digging out from a spreadsheet mess."

**On what they're watching at peer foundations:**
> "My colleague at a larger CF spent two full weeks in December sending out donor year-end summaries manually. She told me 'we should have built this better when we were small.' That's exactly what I'm trying to avoid."

**On the donor relationship:**
> "Our donors are small-business owners and professionals in our community. They chose us — a local community foundation — over Fidelity or Schwab. That's meaningful. They chose us because they trust us with their philanthropic identity. If we can't give them visibility into their giving, we're not delivering on that trust."

**On pricing:**
> "We're small now. If something was free or very low cost to start and scaled with us as we grew, that's the model I'd advocate for. I can't justify $500/month for a 7-account DAF program. But $50/month now with a path to $300/month as we grow — that's a sensible conversation."

**On piloting:**
> "I would be your reference customer. I would write a case study. I would introduce you to everyone I know at other community foundations. We're a nonprofit and we help each other. If this tool works for us, I'll be your ambassador."
