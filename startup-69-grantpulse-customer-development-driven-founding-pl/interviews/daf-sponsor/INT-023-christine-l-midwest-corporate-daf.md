# Interview Transcript — INT-023
## DAF Sponsor — Corporate DAF Program Officer

**Date:** 2025-07-16  
**Duration:** 44 minutes  
**Format:** Zoom  
**Participant:** Christine L., Corporate Responsibility Manager  
**Org:** Midwest Manufacturing Co. (Employee DAF Program)  
**Segment:** DAF Sponsor (Corporate)  
**Fund Count:** 85 employee DAF accounts  
**Grant Volume:** ~180 grants/year  
**Tools:** Spreadsheet + custom internal portal (underpowered), bank transfers  
**Problem Severity:** 8/10  
**Pilot Interest:** 6/10 (IT security review required)  

---

## Key Excerpts

**On the scale problem:**
> "We have 85 employees with DAF accounts through our corporate giving program. They each log recommendations through our internal portal. But the portal was built in 2016 by our IT department and hasn't been updated since. It crashes during high-volume periods like year-end giving season. Last December we had 40 employees trying to submit recommendations in the last week and the system went down for 3 days."

**On grant consistency:**
> "Employees can recommend grants to any 501(c)(3) but our legal team has to verify eligibility. That verification is manual — someone sends the EIN to our legal team, they check against a database, they email back. About 15% of recommendations are to organizations that don't qualify. Catching this manually is time-consuming and the employee experience is bad."

**On donor visibility:**
> "Every year I send every employee a PDF summary of their fund activity. It's 85 custom PDFs, each built from a spreadsheet export, each formatted in Word. I spend two full weeks in January on this. Last year I had a formatting error that went out to 20 employees with wrong numbers. I had to recall and resend 20 PDFs."

**On WTP:**
> "Our program admin budget is $40,000/year. We'd easily spend $12,000–15,000 on a system that eliminated the December crash, automated eligibility screening, and produced donor reports automatically. The current situation has real business risk — employees losing trust in the program."
