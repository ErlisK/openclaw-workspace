# Toggl Track — Competitor Analysis

**URL:** https://toggl.com/track/  
**Analyzed:** April 2026  
**Category:** Time Tracking

## Overview
- **Target User:** Agencies, freelancers, consultants who bill by the hour; team leads tracking utilization
- **Pricing:** Free (up to 5 users), $9/user/month (Starter), $18/user/month (Premium), Enterprise custom
- **Core Value Prop:** Effortless time tracking with powerful reporting to maximize billable hours and productivity

## Key Features
- One-click timer (web, desktop, mobile, browser extension)
- Calendar integration — import Google/Outlook calendar events as time entries
- Automated time tracking (background app/website tracking, user-reviewable)
- 100+ integrations (Jira, Salesforce, Asana, GitHub, etc.)
- Project and client tagging
- Billable/non-billable hours distinction
- Custom reports and dashboards
- Team utilization and capacity views
- Idle detection with optional discard
- Anti-surveillance policy (no screenshots)

## UX Patterns
- **Onboarding:** Sign up → start timer immediately → add project/client later; friction-free start
- **Data Input:** Big "Start Timer" button always visible; timer runs in background; tag project/client after
- **Calendar View:** Most popular feature — side-by-side calendar and time entries; drag-and-drop to fill gaps
- **Reporting:** Summary / Detailed / Weekly views; export to PDF/CSV; shareable report links
- **Mobile:** Full-featured; widget for one-tap start; Apple Watch support

## Strengths
- Best-in-class time tracking UX; extremely low friction
- Calendar inference is a standout feature (reduces manual entry)
- Generous free plan drives adoption
- Anti-surveillance stance resonates with freelancers
- 100+ integrations means it fits into any workflow

## Weaknesses / Gaps
- **No income/payment tracking** — tracks hours but doesn't connect to revenue
- **No "true hourly rate"** — you must manually compare hours vs. invoices in another tool
- **No marketplace context** — no Fiverr/Upwork/Etsy integration
- **No ROI per client or project** — shows time cost but not revenue yield
- **Team-focused features bloat the solo/freelance experience**
- **No income goal setting or target alerts**
- **No scheduling availability view**
- Reporting is detailed but not prescriptive (tells "what" not "what to do")

## UX Flows (Annotated)
1. **Quick timer start:** Click play → type project name → timer runs → click stop → tagged entry saved. Under 10 seconds total.
2. **Calendar import:** Connect Google Calendar → "Convert to time entry" button on each event → bulk approve or edit. Massive time saver.
3. **Reporting flow:** Select date range + filters → Summary view shows hours per project → drill down to entries → export. Clean but no revenue context.

## Relevance to GigAnalytics
- **Opportunity:** Toggl is the gold standard for time tracking but completely ignores the revenue side. GigAnalytics bridges time → money, turning Toggl-style data into actionable hourly rates.
- **Borrow:** Calendar inference for time tracking (key differentiator to replicate), one-tap timer UX, anti-surveillance stance.
- **Differentiate:** Attach earnings to time entries, compute effective $/hr per platform, show when to raise rates.
