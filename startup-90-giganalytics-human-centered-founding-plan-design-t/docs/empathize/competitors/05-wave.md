# Wave Accounting — Competitor Analysis

**URL:** https://www.waveapps.com/  
**Analyzed:** April 2026  
**Category:** Accounting / Invoicing (Free Tier)

## Overview
- **Target User:** Small business owners, solopreneurs, freelancers who want free accounting software
- **Pricing:** Free (core accounting + invoicing), paid add-ons for payroll ($20+/month) and payments (2.9% + $0.60)
- **Core Value Prop:** Professional accounting and invoicing for free — "You didn't start your business to be a bookkeeper"

## Key Features
- Free double-entry accounting (bank reconciliation, chart of accounts)
- Invoice creation, sending, and automated reminders
- Online payment acceptance (credit card, bank transfer)
- Receipt scanning and expense tracking
- Financial reports (P&L, balance sheet, cash flow)
- Payroll (paid add-on)
- Advisor/bookkeeper marketplace
- Mobile app for invoicing and receipts
- 350,000+ small businesses on platform

## UX Patterns
- **Onboarding:** Create account → connect bank → receive first categorized transactions; guided but not opinionated
- **Data Input:** Bank sync is primary; manual entry possible; receipt photo capture
- **Dashboard:** Overdue invoices widget, cash flow graph, net income summary, bills to pay
- **Invoicing Flow:** New invoice → select client → add line items → send → track view/open/pay status
- **Reports:** Standard financial reports with date range filters; not highly visual but comprehensive

## Strengths
- Free tier is genuinely full-featured (not crippled freemium)
- Very accessible language — designed for non-accountants
- Strong invoicing with automated follow-up reminders
- Good mobile app for on-the-go invoicing
- Trusted by 350K+ businesses

## Weaknesses / Gaps
- **No time tracking** — no hourly rate concept
- **No multi-stream analytics** — income sources aren't distinguishable beyond manual tagging
- **No marketplace integrations** — Etsy/Fiverr/Upwork are invisible
- **No pricing intelligence or rate optimization**
- **No scheduling/availability**
- **Revenue model tension** — free product means Wave monetizes payments; may not prioritize analytics depth
- **UI feels dated** compared to newer entrants
- **No benchmark data**

## UX Flows (Annotated)
1. **Invoice payment flow:** Invoice sent → client clicks link → sees professional invoice → pays by card/ACH → Wave deposits in 2 business days. End-to-end clean.
2. **Expense categorization:** Bank transaction appears → click → assign account (expense category) → save. Similar to QBO SE but less intelligent auto-suggest.
3. **Cash flow report:** Select date range → P&L auto-generates → export to CSV/PDF. Reliable but not insightful.

## Relevance to GigAnalytics
- **Opportunity:** Wave's free accounting is table stakes. GigAnalytics doesn't compete on accounting — it adds the "what should I do differently" layer on top.
- **Integration opportunity:** GigAnalytics could import Wave data via CSV/API for freelancers who use Wave for bookkeeping but want analytics.
- **Differentiate on intelligence:** Wave tells you what happened; GigAnalytics tells you what to do next.
