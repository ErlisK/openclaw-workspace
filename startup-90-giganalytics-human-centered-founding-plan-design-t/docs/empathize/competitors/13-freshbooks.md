# FreshBooks — Competitor Analysis

**URL:** https://www.freshbooks.com/  
**Analyzed:** April 2026  
**Category:** Accounting + Invoicing + Time Tracking

## Overview
- **Target User:** Freelancers, self-employed professionals, small businesses; especially service businesses that bill by project
- **Pricing:** Lite $19/month (5 clients), Plus $33/month (50 clients), Premium $60/month (unlimited), Select (custom)
- **Core Value Prop:** "Small business software that makes the hard part easy" — save 553 hours/year, $7K in billable hours

## Key Features
- Professional invoicing with automatic payment reminders
- Time tracking (built-in timers, timesheet entry)
- Expense tracking with receipt capture
- Project profitability tracking
- Client portal for invoice viewing and payment
- Accounting (P&L, balance sheet, tax summaries)
- Estimates and proposals
- Bank reconciliation
- Team collaboration (subcontractors)
- 100+ integrations (Stripe, G Suite, Trello, Asana)
- Mobile app (iOS + Android)

## UX Patterns
- **Onboarding:** Import clients → create first invoice → send; designed for immediate value
- **Dashboard:** Outstanding invoices, total income this month, total expenses, net profit summary
- **Invoicing:** Highly visual; auto-populated with tracked time; late payment reminders automated
- **Time Tracking:** Simple timer + manual timesheet; ties directly to projects and billing
- **Project View:** Hours logged vs. budget, expenses vs. budget, team breakdown

## Strengths
- Best invoicing UX in its class — very professional client experience
- Time tracking is tightly integrated with billing (hours → invoice in 2 clicks)
- Project profitability view is useful for client work
- 30M+ users across 160 countries — very established
- Strong mobile app

## Weaknesses / Gaps
- **Single-client billing model** — assumes you have "clients" in traditional sense; doesn't model marketplace income
- **No multi-platform analytics** — Fiverr/Upwork/Etsy aren't clients in FreshBooks
- **Expensive for solo side-hustlers** — $19-33/month for 5-50 "clients" doesn't map well to marketplace sellers
- **No true hourly rate across income types**
- **No pricing intelligence or peer benchmarking**
- **No availability scheduling**
- **No A/B pricing experiments**

## UX Flows (Annotated)
1. **Time → Invoice:** Track time on project → end of month → "New Invoice" → FreshBooks auto-adds time entries as line items → customize → send. Excellent flow.
2. **Late payment flow:** Invoice sent → auto-reminder at 7/14/21 days → client pays → FreshBooks reconciles. Reduces awkward payment chasing.
3. **Project profitability:** Project page → Hours logged / Hours budgeted gauge → Revenue / Expenses summary → Net profit. Clear for client-billing freelancers.

## Relevance to GigAnalytics
- FreshBooks is the closest to GigAnalytics for traditional freelancers (client billing), but completely misses marketplace-based income.
- **Opportunity:** Convert FreshBooks users who also have Fiverr/Etsy/Upwork income — offer import from FreshBooks CSV.
- **Key gap:** FreshBooks assumes all income comes from invoiced clients. GigAnalytics assumes income comes from anywhere — platforms, invoices, products, services.
