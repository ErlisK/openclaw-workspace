# Empathy Map — Persona 2: The Multi-Gig Creator

## Persona Profile

**Name:** Priya, 27  
**Location:** Chicago, IL (works from home)  
**Gig Mix:** Etsy (handmade jewelry + digital art prints), Fiverr (logo design + Canva templates), Instagram/Gumroad (digital products), Substack (paid newsletter, $300/month)  
**Income Range:** $1,800-$3,500/month gross from 4 income streams; some months wildly variable  
**Tech Comfort:** Medium — comfortable with social media tools, Canva, basic spreadsheets; not a developer  
**Background:** Full-time UX designer at a startup; side hustles started as creative outlets that unexpectedly became significant income  

---

## SAYS

- *"I know I'm making money but I genuinely don't know if my Etsy shop is worth the time I put into it vs. just doing more Fiverr gigs."*
- *"Etsy analytics show me views and sales but not how long each piece took me to make and list."*
- *"I track everything in Google Sheets but my formulas are getting complicated and I'm not confident they're right."*
- *"I made $600 on Etsy in December but I spent 3 weeks prepping inventory. Was that good? I have no idea."*
- *"My Fiverr conversion rate dropped last month but I don't know if it's pricing, my gig thumbnail, or just seasonality."*
- *"I want to raise my Fiverr prices but I'm scared. What if everyone leaves?"*
- *"Tax time is a nightmare. PayPal, Etsy Payments, Stripe for Gumroad — I had four different statements to reconcile."*

## THINKS

- *"My Etsy shop feels like the most visible part of my brand but might actually be the lowest $/hr income."*
- *"I should cut one of my income streams to focus, but what if I cut the wrong one?"*
- *"If only I could see: this stream = $X/hr, this stream = $Y/hr — I'd know immediately what to do."*
- *"I wonder if I'm undercharging on Fiverr compared to designers with similar portfolios."*
- *"The digital products on Gumroad are basically passive — maybe I should put more energy there."*
- *"I'm probably working 30+ hours/week across everything and I'm not sure I'm making more than if I just freelanced directly."*

## DOES

- Takes handmade jewelry photos and lists on Etsy every 2-3 weeks (batch production)
- Logs into 4 different dashboards weekly (Etsy, Fiverr, Gumroad, Substack) to check earnings
- Maintains a master Google Sheet with tabs for each platform; updates it monthly (inconsistently)
- Tracks production time for Etsy in a notes app ("spent 4h on ring batch") — not connected to revenue
- Reads "should I raise my Fiverr prices?" threads on Reddit; acts on vibes, not data
- Posts about her craft on Instagram; unsure if this drives Etsy or Fiverr revenue
- Does taxes with a CPA who charges her extra for the multi-source income complexity

## FEELS

- **Creative fulfillment** from all platforms — the work itself brings joy
- **Financially uncertain** — months vary widely; anxiety about slow months
- **Cognitively overloaded** — managing 4 platforms + a day job stretches her thin
- **Proud but exhausted** — being a "multi-passionate entrepreneur" sounds great until 11pm on a Sunday
- **Frustrated** by not knowing which creative work is "worth it" financially
- **Excited** about potential — feels like she's close to cracking the code but lacks the data to do it

---

## Pains

1. **Platform dashboard overwhelm** — 4 separate analytics screens; no unified view
2. **Production time not tracked** — knows revenue per platform but not time invested
3. **True $/hr is invisible** — Etsy might be $8/hr (after materials + photos + listing time)
4. **Pricing decisions are fear-based** — wants data to confidently raise Fiverr/Etsy prices
5. **Spreadsheet fragility** — custom Google Sheet breaks or gets abandoned; data gap forms
6. **Attribution blindness** — Instagram posts, doesn't know if they drive Etsy or Fiverr traffic
7. **Multi-1099 tax nightmare** — CPA charges premium for multi-platform complexity
8. **Stream prioritization impossible** — can't drop a platform without data showing it's the weakest

## Gains (Desired Outcomes)

1. **One-screen overview** of all 4 platforms — earnings, trend, $/hr per stream
2. **"Which platform is your best investment?" recommendation** — clear data-driven answer
3. **Simple production time logging** — one-tap "I worked on Etsy for 2 hours" that connects to revenue
4. **Pricing confidence** — "Your Fiverr rate is 18% below similar designers; here's a suggested test"
5. **Stream-level decision support** — "Your Gumroad products generate $45/hr passive; double down here"
6. **Tax-ready summary** — single export for accountant

---

## Key Insight for GigAnalytics

Priya experiences **creative energy without financial clarity**. She's building something meaningful but can't see which parts of her multi-platform business are truly worth her limited time. GigAnalytics must surface the "Gumroad passive income revelation" — showing that her digital products earn $45/hr with near-zero time investment vs. Etsy jewelry at $9/hr — in a way that feels empowering, not punishing. The product must celebrate her creative work while giving her the financial intelligence to optimize it.

---

## Design Implications
- **Non-technical UI required** — no jargon, clean visual presentation, mobile-first
- **Production time logging:** Must be nearly frictionless — 2-tap max; suggest based on calendar
- **Etsy + Fiverr + Gumroad + Substack** are the four critical integrations
- **Positive framing:** Show "your best performing stream" not just "your worst"
- **Key action prompt:** "Based on your data, here's what to do this week"
