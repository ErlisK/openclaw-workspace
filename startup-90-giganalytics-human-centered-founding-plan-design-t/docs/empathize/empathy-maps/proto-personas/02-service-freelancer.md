# Proto-Persona 2: The Service Freelancer
### Empathy Map — Think / Feel / See / Do / Pain / Gain

**Persona archetype:** Sells skilled services on an hourly or project basis — design (graphic, UX, web), software development, coaching (business, life, fitness), writing, consulting, marketing. Works across 2-4 income channels simultaneously: a primary marketplace (Upwork/Toptal/Fiverr), direct clients, and potentially a retainer or two.

**Composite profile:**
- Age: 27–40, gender-diverse; often former employee who went freelance
- Location: remote; often US/UK/EU; some international/digital nomad
- Income range: $4,000–$15,000/month gross; depends heavily on utilization
- Tech comfort: Medium-High — comfortable with SaaS tools, spreadsheets, APIs; not necessarily a developer
- Platforms: Upwork (primary), direct clients (email/contract), Toptal (select), LinkedIn (lead gen), Substack or newsletter for thought leadership

---

## 🧠 THINKS

*Internal beliefs, mental models, and internal monologue.*

- *"I know I'm probably undercharging. I just raised rates once and it worked. But I have no idea how high I can go."*
- *"My most profitable client is probably not my biggest client by revenue. I've never actually calculated it."*
- *"I spend 3-4 hours a week writing proposals that don't convert. Is that counted in my hourly rate? It should be."*
- *"Toggl tells me where my time went. It doesn't tell me if that time was worth anything."*
- *"If my direct clients pay better per hour than Upwork, why am I still on Upwork? But I don't know for certain."*
- *"I need to raise my Upwork rate. But my JSS dropped 0.2 points last month and I don't know why, so now feels wrong."*
- *"I track my time in Toggl and my invoices in Wave and my Upwork earnings in a spreadsheet. The systems don't talk."*
- *"Tax season is going to hurt. I have four income sources and they all report differently."*
- *"I want to go full-time on my own. But I don't know what my 'number' is — what monthly income makes it safe?"*
- *"I'm probably worth more to my best client than I charge. But how do I bring that up without damaging the relationship?"*

---

## ❤️ FEELS

*Emotional experiences through work and financial life.*

- **Empowered** by the freedom of freelance — choosing clients, setting schedule, working from anywhere
- **Financially anxious** between contracts or during slow months; the gap is the most stressful part
- **Proud of expertise** but insecure about its market value — imposter syndrome appears when pricing
- **Frustrated** by administrative work — time tracking, invoicing, proposals, chasing payments feel like a tax on their real skills
- **Excited** when a great client or project comes in; **disappointed** when the economics later reveal it wasn't as profitable as hoped
- **Overwhelmed** by running a business AND doing the billable work — "CEO of one" fatigue
- **Competitively anxious** — knows peers exist but has almost no data on how they compare
- **Relieved** during high-income months; **stressed** when two clients leave in the same week
- **Motivated to grow** but unsure which dimension to grow — raise rates? more clients? passive income?
- **Guilty** about non-billable time — every hour on proposals, learning, or admin feels "wasted"

---

## 👁️ SEES

*What they observe in their environment and information landscape.*

- Upwork profile stats: JSS score, profile views, proposal-to-hire ratio — but no explanation for changes
- Toggl weekly time breakdown — hours per project, but zero revenue context
- Stripe or PayPal deposits arriving at irregular intervals; bank account as primary "income signal"
- r/freelance discussions: "How much should a senior UX designer charge?" with wildly varying answers
- LinkedIn content about "how I 4x'd my freelance income" — inspirational but vague on mechanics
- Competitors on Upwork charging 40% more; sometimes winning jobs, sometimes not
- Tools recommended in every freelance newsletter: FreshBooks, Bonsai, QuickBooks — all feel too heavy
- "Invoice overdue" email chains with clients who pay late — a regular feature of the landscape
- Peers at in-person events who mention their rate casually, triggering "am I charging enough?" spirals
- Product Hunt launches of new freelance tools — invariably missing the multi-platform piece
- Accountant's face during January tax review — pained, because "you have six payment sources again"

---

## ✋ DOES

*Observable behaviors — how they actually operate day to day.*

- Starts Toggl when remembering (50-60% of sessions); forgets to stop it at end of day fairly often
- Bills via multiple systems: FreshBooks or Wave for direct clients, Upwork's built-in for marketplace, PayPal for some legacy clients
- Exports Upwork earnings CSV quarterly and pastes into a Google Sheet alongside Stripe/PayPal data — inconsistently
- Checks 3 separate dashboards on Monday morning (Upwork, Stripe, Wave) to get a "sense" of the week
- Writes new proposals for Upwork 2-3x per week; tracks send/hire ratio mentally (rarely formally)
- Sets rates by googling competitors + asking in forums + gut feel; hasn't changed rate in 12+ months
- Builds and rebuilds income tracking spreadsheets; latest version has been untouched for 5 weeks
- Reads r/freelance rate threads regularly — looking for validation or new data points
- Does monthly bank account reconciliation for taxes; misses deductions because records are incomplete
- Schedules availability mentally; overcommits then scrambles; "always busy" but not always profitably busy
- Takes any new client during slow periods, even low-margin ones, because "any income is income"

---

## 😣 PAINS

1. **True $/hr is never calculated** — bills $120/hr but proposal time, revision cycles, onboarding, and client management mean effective rate is $65-80/hr. Never confronted this.
2. **No cross-platform income view** — Upwork + Stripe + PayPal + occasional checks = 4 different reporting systems, no aggregation
3. **Time tool and money tool are completely separate** — Toggl and Wave/Stripe don't connect; must manually bridge
4. **Proposal time is an invisible cost** — 3-5 hours/week writing proposals that convert at 15-25%; this is a real labor cost that never enters the $/hr calculation
5. **No rate benchmark data** — "Am I charging the right amount?" is answered by guessing and hoping; StackExchange Q with 11K views has no satisfying answer
6. **Upwork JSS is a black box** — a 0.2% drop with no explanation causes anxiety and rate-increase paralysis
7. **Client profitability is invisible** — knows some clients are "better" but has no data on which earns more per hour after all overhead
8. **Income gap anxiety** — 2-3 week gaps between project end and next project start cause financial stress; no forward visibility
9. **Administrative overhead feels like working for free** — invoicing, chasing payments, contract admin are real labor with zero billing
10. **Rate increase timing is opaque** — wants to raise rates but fears losing existing clients; no experiment framework
11. **Tax complexity charges money** — CPA or accountant charges premium for multi-source income; $300-600/year in extra accounting fees
12. **Feast-or-famine** — $12K month followed by $4K month, with no predictive signal for what's coming

---

## 🌟 GAINS

1. **Effective $/hr per income stream** — know that direct clients yield $118/hr effective, Upwork yields $72/hr; use this to make one clear allocation decision
2. **Single dashboard** — one app aggregating Upwork + Stripe + PayPal; "here's your total income this week" in 30 seconds
3. **Proposal ROI metric** — "You spent 4.2 hours on proposals this month; 2 converted to $1,800 in revenue. Your proposal $/hr: $214."
4. **Rate confidence via benchmark** — "Senior UX designers with your experience and market earn $95-140/hr. You're at $90/hr — consider testing $115."
5. **Tax export that ends the January dread** — one CSV: all income, all platforms, net of fees, categorized by type
6. **Income projection** — "Based on current pipeline and historical cadence, August is projected at $7,200 ±$900. Your target is $6,000. You're ahead."
7. **Rate experiment feature** — test $115/hr for new Upwork proposals without changing existing client rates; see conversion data in 3 weeks
8. **Client/stream ROI ranking** — "Client A: $118/hr effective. Upwork projects: $72/hr. Recommendation: be more selective on Upwork, grow direct clients."
9. **"Go full-time" clarity** — "You need $6,200/month net to replace your salary. At your current direct client rate, that's 52 billable hours/month. You averaged 61 last quarter. You're ready."
10. **One-stop financial picture** — feels like a real business operator, not someone doing admin on top of real work

## 🎯 GigAnalytics Design Implications for Service Freelancer

| Insight | Feature Required |
|---------|-----------------|
| True $/hr unknown (proposal time uncounted) | "Overhead time" category: proposals, revisions, admin — attributed to stream |
| 3 separate payment platforms | Upwork CSV + Stripe API + PayPal CSV import; unified earnings view |
| Toggl data not connected to money | Toggl API import; time entries matched to income streams |
| Rate benchmark gap | Anonymized benchmark by specialty: "Senior UX designers in your market earn $X-Y" |
| Income gap anxiety | Forward income projection: pipeline + historical pattern → 30/60/90 day view |
| JSS confusion | Contextualize JSS: "0.2% drop is within normal variance; no action needed" |
| Client profitability invisible | Per-client $/hr after all attributed time — not just billable hours |
| Rate experiment desire | A/B pricing for new proposals: control (current) vs. treatment (test rate) |
| "Go full-time" question | Income threshold calculator: "you need X hours at Y rate to replace salary Z" |

**Killer first-session moment for this persona:**
> *"Your effective hourly rate across all streams: Direct clients $118/hr. Upwork marketplace $71/hr. After accounting for 4.1 hrs/week of unbilled proposal and admin time. [Recommendation: Prioritize direct client growth over Upwork volume.]*"
