# Proto-Persona 3: The Platform Gig Worker
### Empathy Map — Think / Feel / See / Do / Pain / Gain

**Persona archetype:** Earns income primarily through labor-marketplace apps: rideshare (Uber/Lyft), food delivery (DoorDash/Instacart/Grubhub), on-demand task services (TaskRabbit/Handy), pet care (Rover/Wag), or micro-task platforms (Amazon Flex, GoShare). Typically runs 2-4 of these simultaneously to maximize hourly income and hedge against any single platform's slowdowns.

**Composite profile:**
- Age: 19–35; economically diverse; often students, recent immigrants, between jobs, or supplementing part-time work
- Location: urban or suburban (drives or delivers in metro area); mobile-first
- Income range: $800–$3,500/month gross across all platforms; highly dependent on hours worked
- Tech comfort: Medium — native smartphone user; comfortable with apps but not spreadsheets or web tools
- Primary device: Android or iPhone; no laptop for most users in this segment
- Key financial goals: Cover rent and bills, pay tuition, build emergency fund; income is instrumental, not identity

---

## 🧠 THINKS

*Internal beliefs, mental models, and continuous internal calculation.*

- *"I know DoorDash is busier than Uber right now, but is it actually paying better per hour? I think so but I'm not sure."*
- *"After gas, my real Uber earnings are way less than what the app says. But I've never done the actual math."*
- *"I should focus on the gig that pays the most per hour. But I don't have good data on which one that is."*
- *"If I work this Saturday on TaskRabbit, I could make $X. If I work Uber, I'll make $Y. I don't actually know those numbers."*
- *"I need $2,000 this month for rent. I'm at $1,100 right now. I need to hustle this week but I'm not sure how many hours that is."*
- *"Gas is costing me way too much but I don't know exactly how much it's eating into my earnings."*
- *"Do I owe taxes on all this? I've been getting 1099s but I don't really know what to do with them."*
- *"That cash job for my neighbor was like $80 for 2 hours. That's way better than driving. I should do more of those."*
- *"I've been doing this for 6 months. Am I making more than I would just working retail? I honestly don't know."*
- *"I want to scale back on Uber because the algorithm has been giving me terrible routes lately. But it's all I can count on when TaskRabbit is slow."*

---

## ❤️ FEELS

*Emotional experience — what drives and deflates this persona.*

- **Self-reliant and capable** — "I made this money by myself, on my own time" — strong identity around hustle and independence
- **Financially uncertain** — lives close to the edge; a slow week can mean real stress about basics
- **Motivated by targets** — rent, a specific bill, tuition — concrete goals drive daily decisions more than long-term vision
- **Frustrated by inefficiency** — waiting for pings, driving to poorly-timed pickups, low-demand windows feel like wasted time
- **Anxious about expenses** — gas, vehicle wear, phone plan, supplies for dog walking all erode earnings but are never accounted for
- **Proud on good days** — a $200 Sunday on TaskRabbit feels like a win; shares earnings with friends
- **Tired on bad days** — 5 hours of driving for $60 net is demoralizing when it happens
- **Curious but overwhelmed** by optimization — knows gig workers share tips ("hit this zone at 5pm"), wants data but doesn't know how to get it
- **Tax anxious** — 1099-K forms from multiple platforms feel like a trap; afraid of doing it wrong
- **Empowered by optionality** — "I can always go drive" is a safety net that feels important even if the ROI is poor

---

## 👁️ SEES

*What's visible in their environment — information inputs, community signals, platform stimuli.*

- **Surge pricing notifications** — the primary decision-making prompt for when to work Uber/Lyft; reactive, not proactive
- **App earnings summary** — each platform shows last 7 days, last month, total; never shows cross-platform view
- **Social media side-hustle content** — TikTok/YouTube: "I make $X/week doing [gig]" — aspirational; gross figures, no real math
- **Peer conversations** — friends who also do gig work compare notes informally: "DoorDash was dead tonight" / "TaskRabbit was great this weekend"
- **Gas prices** — viscerally aware of gas costs; directly affects how they feel about driving shifts
- **Rating scores** — Uber 4.8, DoorDash 95%, Rover 5-star — worried about maintaining scores to stay on platform
- **1099-K forms in January** — one per platform, confusing, multiple amounts; scary and unclear
- **Reddit posts** — r/UberDrivers, r/doordash_drivers, r/sidehustle — anecdotal advice, no personalized data
- **Bank account deposits** — PayPal and direct deposits from 4 platforms; only unified view is their bank app
- **Other drivers' tips** — "surge usually starts at 7pm downtown on Fridays" — community knowledge, personalized to their city but not to their history
- **Platform algorithm changes** — Uber/DoorDash/TaskRabbit algorithm updates reduce earnings with no explanation; notices the drop, can't diagnose it

---

## ✋ DOES

*Observable actions — daily behaviors and interaction patterns.*

- Opens 2-3 gig apps every morning; picks the one with the best surge or notification — reactive, not planned
- Switches apps mid-day based on surge notifications — no systematic strategy
- Checks each platform's weekly earnings summary separately (multiple app opens per day)
- Tracks income mentally or in phone Notes app: "made about $300 this week I think"
- Calculates total income via bank app when bill is due — adds up deposits mentally
- Fills gas tank and notes it as expensive; does not formally track fuel cost against earnings
- Declines low-paying gigs (sub-threshold orders on DoorDash) based on intuition, not calculated $/hr
- Accepts cash jobs from neighbors or word-of-mouth — completely off the grid
- Files taxes manually or via TurboTax — struggles with multi-1099 complexity; sometimes misses deductions
- Talks to friends about which platforms are "worth it" right now — community intelligence is the primary data source
- Takes on new platforms periodically (tries Instacart, Handy, Amazon Flex) when a friend recommends it
- Uses Uber driver app's heat maps (where demand is high) — the best analytical tool currently available to them

---

## 😣 PAINS

1. **True $/hr after expenses is completely unknown** — Uber shows $18/hr gross. After gas ($4-5/hr at current prices), vehicle wear (~$0.18/mile), and waiting/driving-to-zone time, real rate may be $10-12/hr. Never calculated.
2. **No cross-platform income aggregation** — 4 app dashboards, no way to see total income without adding up manually or checking bank
3. **Decision-making based on surge alerts, not data** — chooses which platform to open based on current notifications, not historical $/hr for that time/day
4. **Cash income is invisible** — $80 cash for yard work, $50 for moving help from a neighbor — never enters any record
5. **Expense blindness** — gas, car insurance, phone plan, Rover supplies are all costs of doing business; none tracked or deducted
6. **Income goal tracking is manual** — knows rent is $1,400 but tracks progress toward it mentally; no "you need $X more" tracker
7. **Tax complexity and fear** — multiple 1099-Ks, vehicle deduction available but not claimed, quarterly estimates missed
8. **Platform algorithm opacity** — earnings drop unexpectedly; can't tell if it's a slow period, algorithm change, rating impact, or bad luck
9. **No scheduling strategy** — best times to work are known anecdotally but not personalized to their actual earning history
10. **Comparing platforms is guesswork** — "Is TaskRabbit worth more than Uber?" known vaguely but never confirmed by data
11. **Rating score anxiety** — a single bad rating can threaten platform access; no context for whether a rating is significant or noise
12. **Mobile-only constraint** — any tool requiring a laptop or desktop is completely inaccessible to this persona

---

## 🌟 GAINS

1. **Live income meter** — "You've earned $1,100 toward your $2,000 rent goal. 14 days left. You need to earn $64/day." — visible, real, actionable
2. **True $/hr per platform after expenses** — "Your TaskRabbit earns $26/hr effective. Uber earns $11/hr after gas. Focus on TaskRabbit."
3. **Cross-platform total income view** — one screen showing all 4 apps' earnings, not 4 separate dashboards
4. **Daily recommendation** — "Tuesday 4-7pm: TaskRabbit demand is highest for furniture assembly in your area. Better $/hr than Uber tonight."
5. **Expense quick-log** — one-tap to log a gas fill-up ($52) → automatically modeled against Uber/Lyft earnings; true net recalculated
6. **Tax guidance** — "You may owe ~$400 in Q2 self-employment tax. You can deduct $X in vehicle expenses. Here's what to track." — not scary, actionable
7. **Cash income logging** — one tap: "Cash job — $80 — 2 hours" → tracked, counted toward goal
8. **Platform comparison clarity** — definitive answer to "which gig pays best for me, specifically, based on my own history?"
9. **Goal progress momentum** — progress bar toward income target drives motivation (like Duolingo streak for income)
10. **Peer comparison** — "You earn 14% more per hour than similar drivers in your city. Your best platform choice is better than average." — pride and validation
11. **Mobile-first, 30-second interaction** — everything this persona needs in one screen, on their phone, in the time between gigs

## 🎯 GigAnalytics Design Implications for Platform Gig Worker

| Insight | Feature Required |
|---------|-----------------|
| True $/hr after gas/expenses unknown | Expense quick-log (gas, supplies) auto-deducted from platform earnings |
| 4 separate platform dashboards | Unified view: Uber + DoorDash + TaskRabbit + Rover total income |
| Reactive platform switching (no data) | Personalized heatmap: best $/hr by platform, day, and time of day |
| Income goal tracking is mental | Real-time income goal bar: "$1,100 of $2,000 — 14 days remaining" |
| Cash income completely invisible | "Log cash job" button: amount + hours → adds to total + goal progress |
| Tax 1099-K confusion and missed deductions | Tax guidance: deduction prompts, quarterly estimate, vehicle tracking |
| Mobile-only user | 100% mobile-first UI; home screen widget for income goal and daily recommendation |
| 30-second interaction tolerance | Dashboard must communicate key info within 30 seconds of opening |

**Killer first-session moment for this persona:**
> *"Your effective hourly rate by platform this month: TaskRabbit $26.40/hr | Rover $31.20/hr | Uber $11.80/hr (after gas). You've been spending 65% of your hours on your two lowest-paying platforms.*"

**Pricing note for this persona:** Free tier is essential. Even $4.99/month may be a friction point for users earning $800-1,500/month. Freemium with upgrade incentive (heatmap, tax guidance) is the right model.
