# Proto-Persona 1: The Creator-Seller
### Empathy Map — Think / Feel / See / Do / Pain / Gain

**Persona archetype:** Runs an Etsy shop + sells digital products on Gumroad (or similar: Shopify, Payhip, Ko-fi). May also have a small Substack, social following, or Fiverr design gigs. Creative work is identity, not just income.

**Composite profile:**
- Age: 24–34, often female-skewing
- Location: urban or suburban; frequently works from home alongside a day job
- Income from creative streams: $800–$4,000/month gross; highly variable
- Tech comfort: Medium — fluent with Canva, social media tools, Shopify, basic spreadsheets; not a developer
- Time available for side income: 10–25 hrs/week around full-time job or family

---

## 🧠 THINKS

*Internal beliefs, assumptions, and mental models — what's going on inside their head that they don't necessarily say out loud.*

- *"My Etsy shop is my brand. I feel guilty when I think about slowing it down, even if it's the least profitable thing I do."*
- *"I don't really know if I'm making money. I know sales came in but fees, shipping, materials, and my time make it fuzzy."*
- *"I should raise my prices but what if my regular buyers leave? I don't want to alienate the audience I worked so hard to build."*
- *"My digital products feel 'passive' but they still take days to create and launch. Is that actually passive?"*
- *"If I knew which thing actually made me the most per hour, I'd do more of that. But I've never calculated it properly."*
- *"I'm probably losing money on some orders when you factor in materials and time. But I don't want to know."*
- *"I feel like a real entrepreneur but my 'systems' are a janky Notion doc and a Google Sheet I haven't opened in 6 weeks."*
- *"The tax stuff is going to be a mess again. Four different payment sources, no unified record."*

---

## ❤️ FEELS

*Emotional states — what they experience emotionally throughout their work day and financial life.*

- **Creatively fulfilled** by the work itself — making things for real customers is intrinsically rewarding
- **Financially anxious** — month-to-month income variance causes genuine stress; hard to plan
- **Proud but imposter-syndrome-adjacent** — says "I have a business" but feels like it's barely a business
- **Overwhelmed** by platform sprawl — Etsy, Gumroad, Shopify, Instagram, email list, Substack all demand attention
- **Excited by growth moments** (a viral product, good review, big sales day) but the high fades without clear data
- **Guilty about "unproductive" time** — time spent on low-margin Etsy orders feels like it should be productive
- **Frustrated** by platform opacity — Etsy's fees are "everywhere and nowhere" and hard to compute
- **Relieved** during months when passive digital product income covers most of the target — but can't sustain it intentionally
- **Reluctant to look too closely** at true per-hour earnings — afraid of what she'll find

---

## 👁️ SEES

*What they observe in their environment — tools, communities, content, peers, patterns they notice.*

- Social media feeds full of "I made $X on Etsy this month" content — most showing gross without context
- Successful creator peers with "multiple income streams" as a badge of honor; rarely reveals the complexity
- Notion templates for freelance income tracking showing up in her Twitter/Pinterest feed
- Ads for FreshBooks, QuickBooks, Wave — all feel corporate and over-complicated for her situation
- YouTube videos about Etsy fee changes causing panic in seller communities
- Reddit threads (r/EtsySellers, r/GumroadCreators) full of "is this worth it?" questions with no clear answers
- Fellow creators complaining about Etsy's algorithm changes making sales unpredictable
- Tool overwhelm — a new productivity app mentioned every week in her creator newsletter
- Financial influencers on TikTok/Instagram showing "passive income" without explaining the time investment
- Google Sheets income tracking templates with 15+ columns — intimidating, not motivating

---

## ✋ DOES

*Observable behaviors — what they actually do day-to-day, how they interact with tools, what their routines look like.*

- Checks Etsy dashboard every morning on mobile (orders, views, revenue) — separate from Gumroad check
- Takes production photos and lists new products on Etsy in batches (weekly or bi-weekly)
- Logs into 3-4 dashboards weekly (Etsy, Gumroad, PayPal/Stripe, Substack) to assess performance
- Tracks income in a Google Sheet or Notion database — updated inconsistently (monthly at best)
- Notes production time in a phone memo ("spent 3 hours on that necklace batch") — never connects to revenue
- Exports Etsy finance statement in January for taxes — surprised every year by how complex it is
- Posts about new products on Instagram/Pinterest — tracks post engagement but not conversion to sales
- Reads "should I raise my Etsy prices?" Reddit threads without ever finding a definitive answer
- Compares her prices to top competitors manually (open 5 tabs, compare listings)
- Responds to customer messages daily — doesn't count this as "work time" but it is
- Periodically tries a new "system" (new spreadsheet template, new app) — reverts to old habits within 3 weeks

---

## 😣 PAINS

*Friction points, blockers, and frustrations — what makes this hard, what causes distress.*

1. **Invisible true $/hr** — makes $400 from Etsy in a month but spent 60 hours; that's $6.67/hr before materials. Never calculates this. When she does, it's often devastating.
2. **Etsy fee stack is opaque** — listing fee + transaction fee + payment processing + offsite ads = 25-30% gone before she sees a dollar; never displayed as one number
3. **Production time is untracked** — hours making, photographing, packaging physical goods go uncounted; destroys real profitability
4. **Platform algorithm dependency** — Etsy search ranking changes can cut sales 40% overnight; no data to diagnose why
5. **Cross-platform reconciliation is a monthly ordeal** — Etsy Payments + PayPal + Stripe = 3 CSV exports, 3 formats, manual merge
6. **Pricing paralysis** — afraid to raise prices on high-review items; no data on price elasticity for her market
7. **"Passive income" is a lie she tells herself** — Gumroad products do generate passive income but launch prep, updates, customer support eat 8-10 hours/launch
8. **Spreadsheet abandonment cycle** — builds tracker → uses it 3 weeks → stops → guilt → builds new one → repeat
9. **No forward visibility** — can't answer "will I hit $2K this month?" until the month ends
10. **Material cost tracking** — physical product cost basis is almost never systematically tracked
11. **Customer service time is free** — responding to "when does my order ship?" messages not counted anywhere
12. **Income identity crisis** — "Am I a creator or a business owner? Do I need an accountant?"

---

## 🌟 GAINS

*Desired outcomes and success states — what does "winning" look like for this persona?*

1. **One-screen income overview** — open one app, see Etsy + Gumroad + Shopify in one view, in under 30 seconds
2. **True $/hr revelation per product type** — know that digital downloads earn $45/hr and physical jewelry earns $7/hr → make intentional allocation choices
3. **Fee transparency without math** — see net take-home per sale automatically, with fee breakdown on demand
4. **Confident pricing** — have data to support raising prices ("similar digital art packs sell at $18-25; yours are at $9")
5. **Production time link to revenue** — quick log "2 hours on jewelry batch" → auto-connects to Etsy earnings for that batch → true $/hr per product type
6. **Tax export in January that doesn't cause panic** — one file, all income, categorized, CPA-ready
7. **Monthly income goal progress bar** — "You've hit 63% of your $2,500 target with 12 days left"
8. **Passive vs. active income ratio** — see that Gumroad is truly passive (0.2 hrs/month per $100 earned) vs. Etsy physical (12 hrs/month per $100 earned)
9. **Stream-exit data** — clear evidence if/when to pause the Etsy physical shop and focus entirely on digital products
10. **Creative confidence + financial intelligence in same product** — doesn't want to become an accountant; wants the numbers to be handled so she can focus on creating

## 🎯 GigAnalytics Design Implications for Creator-Seller

| Insight | Feature Required |
|---------|-----------------|
| True $/hr on physical vs. digital products | Stream-level $/hr after production time + fees |
| Etsy fee opacity | Auto-apply Etsy fee model (6.5% + 3% + $0.20 + optional offsite ads %) |
| Production time never tracked | Quick-log widget: "2 hours on Etsy batch" → assigned to Etsy stream |
| Spreadsheet abandonment | Zero-maintenance auto-import from Etsy OAuth + Gumroad API |
| Platform comparison | Side-by-side stream ROI: digital vs. physical vs. service |
| Pricing paralysis | Category benchmark: "similar digital art in your price range sells at $X median" |
| Tax ordeal | One-click annual export: all income, net of fees, by source |
| "Should I quit Etsy?" question | Stream ROI ranking with explicit "is this worth your time?" framing |

**Killer first-session moment for this persona:**
> *"Your Gumroad digital products earn $41/hr effective. Your Etsy handmade items earn $6.80/hr effective after materials and production time. [Recommendation: Focus growth energy on digital products.]*"
