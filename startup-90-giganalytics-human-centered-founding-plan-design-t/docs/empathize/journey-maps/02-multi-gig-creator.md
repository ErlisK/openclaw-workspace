# Journey Map — Persona 2: The Multi-Gig Creator (Priya)

## Overview
**Persona:** Priya, 27 — Etsy shop + Fiverr design gigs + Gumroad digital products + Substack  
**Journey:** Overwhelmed multi-platform creator finds clarity and optimizes her creative business  
**Timeframe:** Discovery → 6-month optimization arc

---

## Stage 1: Awareness — "I'm drowning in dashboards"
**Timeframe:** Before GigAnalytics  

- **Trigger:** It's Sunday night. Priya opens 4 browser tabs — Etsy, Fiverr, Gumroad, Substack — to see how the week went. She closes them all feeling more confused than when she started. "I need one place for all of this."
- **Actions:** 
  - Googles "tool to track income from Etsy Fiverr Gumroad"
  - Finds a Notion template — downloads it, spends 2 hours setting it up
  - Realizes it requires manual entry for every transaction
  - Closes Notion; back to square one
- **Thoughts:** "Everything requires me to do the work. No tool actually connects to these platforms automatically."
- **Feelings:** Exhausted, defeated, but determined to find something
- **Pain Points:** Notion's manual entry is a dealbreaker for a person managing 4 active platforms
- **GigAnalytics Opportunity:** "Automatic import from Etsy, Fiverr, Gumroad — no manual entry" is the headline that converts her

---

## Stage 2: Discovery — "Wait, this does the automatic import?"
**Timeframe:** Day 1  

- **Trigger:** Sees GigAnalytics mentioned in a Substack post about freelance tools, or a Google result for "Etsy Fiverr income tracker automatic"
- **Actions:** 
  - Reads landing page slowly — scrolls past feature list to the platform logos (sees Etsy and Fiverr listed)
  - "Does this really auto-import from Etsy?" — looks for evidence
  - Finds a short demo video showing Etsy OAuth connection → instant transaction sync
  - Signs up for free trial
- **Thoughts:** "If this actually works, I'll pay for it forever."
- **Feelings:** Cautiously hopeful (she's been burned by overpromising tools)
- **Pain Points:** Trust gap — unclear how much manual work is still required
- **GigAnalytics Opportunity:** The demo video + "connected in 2 minutes" social proof is the conversion moment

---

## Stage 3: Onboarding — "This is actually magic"
**Timeframe:** Days 1-5  

- **Actions:**
  - Connects Etsy via OAuth (2 minutes) → 6 months of transactions instantly imported
  - Uploads Fiverr CSV (30 seconds) → mapped to income stream
  - Connects Gumroad via API key (1 minute)
  - Adds Substack revenue manually (she manually transfers earnings)
  - Sees for the first time: all 4 platforms in one earnings graph
- **"Wow" moment:** GigAnalytics shows: *"Your Gumroad digital products earn $38/hr (passive). Your Etsy handmade jewelry earns $7.20/hr (after estimated production time). Your Fiverr design earns $24/hr."*
- **Thoughts:** "My Etsy shop is actually my worst-paying stream. I've been pouring energy into it because it feels most like my 'brand' but it's robbing me."
- **Feelings:** **Shocked and relieved** — an uncomfortable truth but finally actionable
- **Pain Points:** Production time for Etsy was estimated (she hadn't logged it); prompts her to confirm estimates
- **GigAnalytics Opportunity:** The "uncomfortable insight" moment creates immediate retention. She WILL tell her creative friends about this.

---

## Stage 4: Adjustment — "I'm changing how I allocate my time"
**Timeframe:** Month 1-2  

- **Actions:**
  - Reduces Etsy new product launches from weekly to monthly
  - Creates 5 new Gumroad digital templates (high $/hr returns)
  - Raises Fiverr logo design rate from $75 to $100 (using A/B test feature)
  - Logs production time via one-tap timer while crafting Etsy jewelry batches
- **Thoughts:** "I'm doing more Gumroad work and it doesn't feel like work — it's still creative but way more efficient."
- **Feelings:** Energized — feels like she's working smarter; creative work now also feels financially validated
- **Pain Points:** A/B test on Fiverr pricing takes 3 weeks to get statistical significance; she's impatient
- **GigAnalytics Opportunity:** "Your Fiverr A/B test is progressing — 12 more orders needed for confidence" visualization keeps her engaged

---

## Stage 5: Sharing — "I told everyone about this"
**Timeframe:** Month 2-3  

- **Actions:**
  - Posts her income breakdown on her Substack ("How I learned my Etsy shop was my worst business decision")
  - Links to GigAnalytics in the post
  - Her Substack audience (800 subscribers) drives ~40 signups
  - GigAnalytics referral program gives her 1 month free
- **Thoughts:** "I built an audience around creative entrepreneurship — they need to hear this."
- **Feelings:** Influential and helpful to her community; proud of her own data-driven transformation
- **GigAnalytics Opportunity:** This is the viral loop. Creators with audiences will generate organic referrals. Build a referral program and a shareable "income breakdown" card.

---

## Stage 6: Retention / Churn Risk
**Timeframe:** Month 4+  

- **Retention Drivers:**
  - Monthly income breakdown becomes a habit (like checking her bank account)
  - Fiverr pricing intelligence → continued rate optimization
  - Product mix optimization advice ("Your Christmas collection had 3x your normal $/hr — replicate it")
  - Tax export in January
- **Churn Risks:**
  - If Etsy OAuth breaks or Gumroad changes API (integration reliability is critical)
  - If she fully pivots away from Etsy (loses one of the key value props)
  - Pricing — she's on a budget; $15/month must feel worth it
- **Retention Strategy:**
  - Monthly "creator income report" email (personalized)
  - Seasonal alerts ("Holiday prep: your Etsy jewelry peak was Oct 15 last year — start now")
  - Gumroad product performance reports drive ongoing optimization
