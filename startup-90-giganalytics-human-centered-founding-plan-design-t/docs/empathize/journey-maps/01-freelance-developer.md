# Journey Map — Persona 1: The Freelance Developer (Marcus)

## Overview
**Persona:** Marcus, 32 — Full-stack developer on Upwork, Toptal, and direct clients  
**Journey:** Discovering → Trying → Using → Optimizing GigAnalytics  
**Timeframe:** Week 1 → Month 6

---

## Stage 1: Awareness — "I have a problem I haven't named yet"
**Timeframe:** Before GigAnalytics discovery  

- **Trigger:** Marcus exports his Upwork CSV for taxes, opens it in Excel, and realizes he has no idea what his actual hourly rate is across all his work. He spends 2 hours trying to calculate it and gives up.
- **Actions:** Searches "how to track freelance income across multiple platforms" → lands on r/freelance → posts asking for tool recommendations → gets 12 different tools suggested, none of which does exactly what he needs
- **Thoughts:** "Why is there no tool that just shows me $/hr per platform? This seems like such an obvious need."
- **Feelings:** Frustrated, slightly overwhelmed, but newly curious that a solution might exist
- **Pain Points:** Search results are SEO spam; no clear "best tool for this exact problem"
- **GigAnalytics Opportunity:** Be the #1 result for "true hourly rate across freelance platforms" — SEO, Reddit presence, and tool roundup mentions are acquisition levers

---

## Stage 2: Consideration — "Let me try this thing"
**Timeframe:** Day 1 of discovery  

- **Trigger:** Sees GigAnalytics mentioned in r/freelance or a Google result with headline "Your true hourly rate across all platforms"
- **Actions:** Visits landing page → reads the "true hourly rate" explainer → sees Upwork + Stripe + Toggl logos → clicks "Try Free"
- **Thoughts:** "If this actually does what it says in 5 minutes, I'll pay for it."
- **Feelings:** Skeptical but intrigued — he's been burned by tools that overpromise before
- **Pain Points:** Long onboarding flows that require lots of setup before showing value
- **GigAnalytics Opportunity:** Show value in < 5 minutes. Upwork CSV upload → "Your Upwork effective hourly rate is $87/hr (after 10% fee + estimated proposal time)" within 2 minutes of signup

---

## Stage 3: Onboarding — "OK, this is actually working"
**Timeframe:** Days 1-7  

- **Trigger:** Successfully uploads Upwork CSV and connects Stripe via OAuth
- **Actions:** 
  - Sees first unified earnings view (both platforms in one graph)
  - Adds time estimates for Toggl via OAuth connection
  - Sees true hourly rate calculated for the first time: Stripe clients $145/hr, Upwork clients $79/hr
  - "Oh wow" moment: direct clients are almost 2x more valuable per hour
- **Thoughts:** "This is exactly what I needed. Why didn't this exist before?"
- **Feelings:** **Relieved and excited** — has clarity for the first time; wants to explore more
- **Pain Points:** Some time entries are missing (forgot to track in Toggl); app prompts to fill estimates
- **GigAnalytics Opportunity:** The "wow moment" drives word-of-mouth. Build a shareable "My True Hourly Rate" card for social sharing.

---

## Stage 4: Daily/Weekly Use — "This is now part of my workflow"
**Timeframe:** Month 1-3  

- **Actions:**
  - Checks GigAnalytics dashboard weekly (Monday morning review)
  - Uses one-tap timer when starting client work (mobile widget)
  - Receives weekly email "Your top stream last week: Direct Client at $148/hr"
  - Sets income goal for Q2 ($45,000) with milestone tracking
- **Thoughts:** "I'm turning down the Upwork job type that pays worst. Data made this decision easy."
- **Feelings:** More in control; less anxious about rate decisions; pricing discussions with clients feel grounded
- **Pain Points:** Wants to benchmark his rates against other React developers (not yet available)
- **GigAnalytics Opportunity:** Benchmark data feature launch creates strong retention incentive

---

## Stage 5: Growth & Optimization — "I'm using this to make real decisions"
**Timeframe:** Month 3-6  

- **Actions:**
  - Uses A/B pricing feature to test $150/hr vs. $175/hr for new Upwork proposals over 4-week period
  - Benchmark data shows he's 12% below median for senior full-stack developers in Austin market
  - Raises rates across all platforms with confidence
  - Shares "My GigAnalytics year in review" to LinkedIn — drives 3 new direct client inquiries
- **Thoughts:** "I made $15K more this year than last year and I directly attribute $8K of that to the rate increases that GigAnalytics gave me data to make."
- **Feelings:** Empowered; data-driven; advocates for the product with freelance friends

---

## Stage 6: Retention / Churn Risk — "Am I still getting value?"
**Timeframe:** Month 6+  

- **Retention Drivers:**
  - Weekly digest emails with personalized insights keep him engaged between major decisions
  - Year-over-year comparison view shows clear growth
  - Benchmark data updates provide ongoing value
  - Tax export in January — high-value annual moment
- **Churn Risks:**
  - If benchmark data isn't relevant to his specialty (React/Node)
  - If calendar inference doesn't improve and he has to manually log too much
  - If a competitor (Toggl, Bonsai) adds multi-platform analytics
- **GigAnalytics Retention Strategy:**
  - Annual "Year in Review" email (shareable)
  - Tax export reminder (January)
  - New benchmark data refresh (quarterly)
  - Feature release cadence maintains "what's new?" curiosity
