# Journey Map — Persona 3: The Service Juggler (DeShawn)

## Overview
**Persona:** DeShawn, 24 — Uber/Lyft + TaskRabbit + Rover + cash jobs  
**Journey:** Income-maximizing gig worker gains clarity on his most valuable hours  
**Timeframe:** Discovery → 3-month optimization  

---

## Stage 1: Awareness — "I'm working all the time but not getting ahead"
**Timeframe:** Before GigAnalytics  

- **Trigger:** DeShawn does a rough calculation in his head at end of month: made about $1,800, worked roughly 70 hours. "$25/hr — that's... fine?" But he knows his Uber earnings are inflated by tips and he spent $280 on gas. Actual rate is probably closer to $18/hr. And he worked hard on some TaskRabbit jobs that seemed to pay way better, but he doesn't know for sure.
- **Actions:** 
  - Mentions to a friend he wishes there was "one app to see all my gig earnings"
  - Friend sends him a link to GigAnalytics they saw on Reddit
- **Thoughts:** "An app that connects to Uber AND TaskRabbit AND Rover? That would be insane."
- **Feelings:** Curious but skeptical — "another app I'll use for a week and forget"
- **Pain Points:** Already has too many apps on his phone; skeptical of anything requiring setup
- **GigAnalytics Opportunity:** Mobile-first landing page; "Connected to Uber in 30 seconds" claim with a 15-second video showing the process

---

## Stage 2: Discovery — "OK, let me try this"
**Timeframe:** Day 1 (mobile)  

- **Trigger:** Friend shares the link. DeShawn taps it on his phone between rides.
- **Actions:**
  - Scrolls landing page (mobile) → sees Uber, Lyft, TaskRabbit, Rover logos
  - Signs up with Google account (no email required)
  - Tutorial: "Connect your first platform" → taps Uber → OAuth screen → approves → syncs 90 days of data
- **Thoughts:** "That was literally 30 seconds. Let me see what it shows."
- **Feelings:** Surprised it was that easy; engaged by the immediate data
- **Pain Points:** Rover doesn't have an API — prompts to connect via CSV or manual entry
- **GigAnalytics Opportunity:** Even partial data (just Uber) is enough for initial "wow" moment. Don't require all platforms before showing value.

---

## Stage 3: Onboarding — "Wait, I'm making how much per hour on TaskRabbit?"
**Timeframe:** Day 1-3  

- **Actions:**
  - Uber data synced → shows $1,240 from Uber in last 30 days
  - Taps "Add more" → connects Lyft CSV → $280 added
  - Adds TaskRabbit (CSV or manual) → $380 for 14 hours
  - Logs Rover (manual, 8 jobs) → $240 for 6 hours
- **"Wow" Moment:** 
  - GigAnalytics shows: *Uber/Lyft: $16.40/hr (after estimated gas). TaskRabbit: $27.10/hr. Rover: $40/hr.*
  - **DeShawn:** "I've been spending 60% of my time on Uber. It's my lowest-paying gig. I need to flip that."
- **Thoughts:** "I need to hustle more Rover and TaskRabbit bookings and treat Uber as fallback only."
- **Feelings:** Surprised, slightly frustrated at himself, but immediately actionable
- **Pain Points:** Gas expense estimation was approximate — prompts to input actual gas spending; he logs it in 2 taps

---

## Stage 4: Optimization — "I'm choosing my work differently now"
**Timeframe:** Month 1-2  

- **Actions:**
  - Sets "Monthly Target: $2,400" in GigAnalytics
  - Home screen widget shows "Month Progress: $1,100 of $2,400 — 14 days remaining"
  - Gets push notification: "TaskRabbit demand is high in Midtown this weekend — good time to list availability"
  - Declines a slow Uber shift, picks up a TaskRabbit furniture assembly job instead
  - Total income this month: $2,380 (first time over $2,000 target)
- **Thoughts:** "The app is basically telling me when and where to work. I just need to follow it."
- **Feelings:** More intentional about his time; progress bar is motivating (like a game)
- **Pain Points:** Cash jobs from neighbors still invisible; logs them manually but finds the tap flow acceptable

---

## Stage 5: Habit Formation — "I check this every morning"
**Timeframe:** Month 2-3  

- **Actions:**
  - Opens GigAnalytics before deciding which app to use each morning
  - Uses it to understand tax obligations for the first time ("You may owe ~$380 in self-employment tax this quarter")
  - Shares the app with 2 friends who also drive
- **Thoughts:** "This is the closest thing I have to a financial advisor. It just tells me what to do."
- **Feelings:** More financially confident; feels like an adult with a plan
- **GigAnalytics Opportunity:** "Bring a friend" referral with cash reward resonates with this demographic

---

## Stage 6: Retention / Churn Risk
**Timeframe:** Month 3+  

- **Retention Drivers:**
  - Daily income meter → habit-forming, like checking steps on a fitness app
  - Monthly "you beat your goal" notification → positive reinforcement
  - Quarterly tax estimate → genuine financial guidance, not available elsewhere for free
- **Churn Risks:**
  - **Pricing** — even $8/month might be reconsidered if income drops; a free tier is essential
  - If Uber changes API access or data format
  - If a competitor app (like a gig company's own bundled tool) emerges
- **Retention Strategy:**
  - Free tier with core features; upgrade for premium (benchmarks, A/B pricing, projections)
  - Milestone celebrations ("You crossed $2,000/month for the first time!")
  - Peer comparison ("You earn 14% more per hour than similar drivers in Atlanta")
