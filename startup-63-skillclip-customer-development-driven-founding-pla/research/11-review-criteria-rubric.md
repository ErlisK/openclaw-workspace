# CertClip — Mentor Review Criteria & Rubric
**Concierge Pilot — Journeyman Review Standards**
Version: 1.0 | Date: 2026-04-07

---

## Overview

This rubric defines how vetted journeyman mentors evaluate tradesperson work-sample clips on CertClip. Every review produces:

1. **Overall rating** (1–5 stars)
2. **Skill level assessment** (Apprentice / Journeyman / Master)
3. **Timestamped notes** (specific moments in the clip)
4. **Code compliance pass/fail** with jurisdiction notes
5. **Written feedback** (minimum 3 sentences)
6. **Issued badge** (auto-generated on completion)

---

## Dimension 1: Safety Practices

**Weight: 25% of overall assessment**

Evaluate whether the worker follows proper safety protocols throughout the clip.

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| **5** | PPE is complete and appropriate for the hazard. Pre-task safety check demonstrated. No unsafe acts observed. |
| **4** | PPE mostly present. Minor safety observation but no hazardous acts. |
| **3** | Some PPE missing or safety steps skipped but no immediate danger. |
| **2** | Multiple safety violations observed. Would require coaching before continuing work. |
| **1** | Unsafe act that would cause injury or fail a site safety audit. Job-stop level violation. |

**Common safety items by trade:**

| Trade | What to check |
|---|---|
| Electrician | Electrical PPE (rated gloves, arc flash rating), voltage verified dead before working, lockout/tagout visible, rubber matting present |
| Plumber | Eye protection during soldering/chemical work, correct use of heat shield, gas leak detection before lighting, MSDS awareness for chemicals |
| HVAC | PPE for refrigerant handling (safety glasses, gloves), recovery equipment per EPA 608, no venting of refrigerant |
| Welder | Welding helmet correct shade, fire-resistant clothing, ventilation adequate, gas cylinder secured, hot pass identification |
| Pipefitter | Fall protection if elevated, correct PPE for thermal/chemical pipe content, proper tool use for pipe cutting |

---

## Dimension 2: Workmanship Quality

**Weight: 30% of overall assessment**

Evaluate the quality of the actual work demonstrated — not just safety but skill execution.

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| **5** | Work is at the level of an experienced journeyman or master. Clean, methodical, proper technique throughout. Would pass any inspection. |
| **4** | Good quality work with minor imperfections that would not cause failure. Shows competence. |
| **3** | Acceptable quality but notable technique issues or inconsistencies. Needs supervision on complex work. |
| **2** | Several workmanship deficiencies that could cause failure or rework. Needs training. |
| **1** | Work quality is unacceptable. Would fail inspection or cause system failure. |

**Trade-specific workmanship indicators:**

**Electrician:**
- Wire prep (stripping length, insulation damage)
- Termination quality (torque, ring lugs seated, no nicked conductors)
- Conduit work (bends clean, supports adequate, locknut seated)
- Panel organization (wire management, labeling, clearances)
- Color coding and identification

**Plumber:**
- Joint preparation (cleaned, fluxed, proper gap)
- Soldering/brazing quality (full penetration, no drips or voids)
- PEX fittings (ring fully seated, expansion complete)
- Support spacing per code
- Valve installation (orientation, accessible)

**HVAC:**
- Refrigerant handling technique
- Gauge manifold connection (no leaks, proper hose use)
- Vacuum procedures (pull depth, hold time)
- Commissioning sequence completeness
- Documentation habits

**Welder:**
- Joint preparation (gap, bevel angle, cleanliness)
- Arc start and stop quality
- Travel speed consistency
- Bead profile (width, height, undercutting, overlap)
- Interpass temperature management

**Pipefitter:**
- Fit-up precision (gap, alignment)
- Tack weld placement and quality
- Support and alignment methods
- Torque values on flanged joints
- Gasket installation technique

## Dimension 3: Code Compliance

**Weight: 25% of overall assessment**

Evaluate whether the work shown complies with the applicable code standard for the jurisdiction.

### Reviewer Responsibilities
- Identify the applicable code standard for the clip's region
- Flag any visible violations with specific code section reference
- Note if the clip is in a jurisdiction you are not familiar with

### Primary Code Standards by Trade and Region

| Trade | Jurisdiction | Code Standard | Key Sections |
|---|---|---|---|
| Electrician | US National | NEC 2020 / 2023 | 110.14 (terminations), 210.8 (GFCI), 250 (grounding), 310 (conductors), 358 (EMT) |
| Electrician | California | NEC 2022 + Title 24 | Title 24 Part 6 Sections 110–180 (controls, lighting, energy) |
| Electrician | New York City | NYC Electrical Code (NEC 2011 + amendments) | Local Law 88 (sub-metering), Local Law 97 (energy monitoring) |
| Electrician | Chicago | Chicago Electrical Code | City-specific amendments to NEC |
| Plumber | US National | IPC 2021 / UPC | IPC 305 (supports), 312 (tests), 605 (water supply), 602 (fixtures) |
| Plumber | Illinois | Illinois Plumbing Code (IPC-based) | Chicago Plumbing Code local amendments |
| Plumber | Medical Gas | NFPA 99 | Chapter 5 (piped medical gas), 5.1.10 (brazing), 5.1.12 (testing) |
| HVAC | US National | IMC 2021 / EPA 608 | EPA Section 608 (refrigerant handling), ASHRAE 15 (safety) |
| HVAC | Commercial | ASHRAE 62.1 (ventilation), ASHRAE 90.1 (energy) | Minimum ventilation rates, energy efficiency |
| HVAC | Commissioning | ASHRAE Guideline 0 / ASHRAE 135 (BACnet) | Commissioning process, BAS naming conventions |
| Welder | Structural | AWS D1.1 | Chapter 4 (qualifications), Chapter 5 (fabrication), Chapter 6 (inspection) |
| Welder | Piping | ASME Section IX | QW-100 (general), QW-200 (WPS/PQR), QW-400 (variables) |
| Pipefitter | Process piping | ASME B31.3 | Chapter II (design), Chapter VI (inspection), Table 341.3.2 (weld tolerances) |
| Pipefitter | Power piping | ASME B31.1 | Chapter III (materials), Chapter V (fabrication) |

### Code Compliance Pass/Fail Criteria

**PASS:** No visible code violations. Minor best-practice recommendations may be noted but do not affect compliance.

**FAIL:** One or more visible code violations that would cause an inspection failure or create a safety hazard. Document the specific code section violated.

**CONDITIONAL PASS:** Work appears mostly compliant but incomplete information makes a definitive call impossible. Flag as pass with a note explaining what additional documentation is needed.

## Dimension 4: Task Completeness

**Weight: 20% of overall assessment**

Evaluate whether the clip demonstrates a complete or meaningfully representational portion of the task.

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| **5** | The clip shows a complete task execution or a clearly defined phase of a larger task. Beginning, middle, and verification steps visible. |
| **4** | Most of the task is shown. Minor steps missing but sufficient for skill assessment. |
| **3** | Key portions of the task are shown but several important steps are missing. |
| **2** | Only a fragment of the task is shown. Cannot fully assess skill level. |
| **1** | Clip is too short or shows the wrong portion of the task to make any meaningful assessment. |

## Skill Level Definitions

### Apprentice
- Learning core techniques under supervision
- Understands basic safety requirements
- Executes common tasks with guidance
- **Indicators:** Some hesitation in technique, references needed, tool handling still developing, occasional mistakes
- **Expected experience:** 0–3 years in trade

### Journeyman
- Independently executes all standard tasks in the trade
- Strong code knowledge for their jurisdiction
- Efficient and methodical without supervision
- **Indicators:** Confident technique, anticipates next steps, catches own errors, clean workmanship
- **Expected experience:** 3–10 years post-apprenticeship

### Master
- Expert-level execution across all task types including complex specialty work
- Teaches others, identifies subtle compliance issues
- Goes beyond minimum code requirements
- **Indicators:** Exceptional technique, explains reasoning during work, addresses edge cases, demonstrates professional standards that exceed code
- **Expected experience:** 10+ years, specialty certifications, instructing/supervising experience

## Timestamped Note Guidelines

Good timestamped notes make the review credible and actionable.

### Format
`[MM:SS] — [Observation]`

### What makes a good timestamped note
- **Specific:** "0:22 — wire stripping shows correct 5/8 inch strip per NEC 314.28" not "good technique"
- **Code-referenced when relevant:** Include the specific section (e.g., "NEC 110.14(D)")
- **Constructive:** Even on mistakes, explain what should be done instead
- **Brief:** 1-2 sentences max per note

### Recommended: 3–6 notes per clip
- 1–2 positive observations (what the reviewer should keep doing)
- 1–2 areas for improvement
- 1–2 code compliance observations (pass or flag)

### Examples of strong notes

| Timestamp | Note Type | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 0:12 | Positive | "0:12 — Good: voltage verified dead with non-contact tester before opening panel per NFPA 70E Table 130.7(C)(15)" |
| 0:34 | Improvement | "0:34 — Ring lug seated but conductor should be torqued to manufacturer spec per NEC 110.14(D) — show this step" |
| 0:51 | Code flag | "0:51 — Conduit support spacing exceeds 10 feet — NEC 358.30(A) requires support within 3 feet of every box and every 10 feet maximum" |
| 1:05 | Safety | "1:05 — Hot work without welding blanket protection for adjacent combustibles — per OSHA 1926.352(d)" |

## Overall Rating Calculation

The final rating is a weighted average:

```
Overall = (Safety × 0.25) + (Workmanship × 0.30) + (Code Compliance × 0.25) + (Completeness × 0.20)
```

**Rounding:** Round to nearest whole number for the 1–5 star display.

**Exception:** Any safety violation rated 1 automatically caps the overall rating at 2 stars, regardless of other scores.

## Written Feedback Guidelines

### Minimum requirements
- At least 3 sentences
- At least one positive observation
- At least one specific improvement recommendation
- Code reference if compliance issue identified

### Tone guidance
- **Be direct:** Tradespeople want honest feedback, not praise that obscures real issues
- **Be specific:** "Your conduit bends were clean and fell within NEC 358.24 radius requirements" beats "good job"
- **Be constructive:** Critique the work, not the person. "The knockouts were left rough" not "you did the knockouts wrong"
- **Reference code sections:** "Per NEC 312.5..." or "ASME IX QW-202 requires..." — this is what makes CertClip different from YouTube comments

### Length
- Minimum: 50 words
- Target: 100–200 words
- Maximum: 400 words (reviewers get paid per review, not per word)

## Conflict of Interest Policy

Mentors may not review clips from:
- Their own current employees or apprentices
- Workers they have a financial relationship with
- Workers they have a personal relationship with that could bias the review

If a conflict exists, the reviewer should skip the clip in the queue. We will be adding a "skip/conflict" button to the review interface.

---

## Review Turnaround Standard

- **Target:** Complete review within 48 hours of claiming a clip
- **Maximum:** 72 hours (after which the clip is released back to the queue)
- **Minimum time per review:** 15 minutes (system enforces this to ensure genuine reviews)

## Quality Assurance

A sample of 10% of all reviews will be audited by the CertClip team against the rubric.

**Review quality score:** Based on:
- Timestamped note specificity
- Code references accuracy
- Feedback actionability
- Skill level consistency with objective criteria

Mentors with quality scores below 3.5/5.0 after 10 reviews will be contacted for recalibration.
