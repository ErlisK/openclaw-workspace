# SkillClip / CertClip — Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) Definitions
**Phase 1: Customer Discovery A**
Date: 2026-04-07

---

## ICP Framework
We define two primary ICPs (demand and supply sides) and one secondary ICP for launch.

---

## ICP-1: The Skeptical GC (Primary Demand Side)

### Role
**Title:** Owner / Project Manager / Field Superintendent  
**Industry:** Residential and light commercial general contractor, electrical subcontractor, HVAC subcontractor  
**Function:** Directly responsible for hiring tradespeople and deploying them on job sites

### Company Profile
- **Size:** 10–75 employees (or $2M–$15M annual revenue)
- **Structure:** Usually family-owned or owner-operated, 1–3 active job sites simultaneously
- **Hiring volume:** 3–15 new trade hires per year, frequent subcontractor use
- **Tech stack:** Minimal; maybe QuickBooks, a basic scheduling app; no formal ATS

### Trade Focus
- **Primary:** Electrical (most regulated, highest inspection risk)
- **Secondary:** Plumbing, HVAC, mechanical
- **Why these:** Highest code-compliance burden, highest liability for inspector failures

### Region / Jurisdiction (Priority)
| Region | Why Priority |
|---|---|
| **California (Bay Area, LA, San Diego)** | Title 24, strict local amendments, high inspection frequency |
| **Texas (Houston, DFW, Austin)** | Volume of construction; fast growth; diverse labor sourcing |
| **Illinois (Chicago Metro)** | Strong union presence, strict city permits, good mentor supply |
| **New York (NYC, Long Island)** | Local Law 97, complex permit regime |
| **Ontario, Canada** | OESC/ESA jurisdiction; strong trades culture |

### Urgency Trigger
The ICP-1 is **actively hiring** or has been burned in the past 6 months by a bad hire, a failed inspection, or a contractor who misrepresented credentials.

Urgency is HIGH when:
- They have a project starting in <30 days and need verified workers
- They just had an inspection failure and are looking to prevent recurrence
- They lost a bid because they couldn't prove their crew's compliance credentials

### Current Workarounds
1. Reference calls to previous employers (slow, biased, informal)
2. Requiring paper license copies + state board verification (takes days)
3. Trial period on low-stakes tasks (expensive, wastes time)
4. Relying on known subcontractors / personal network (doesn't scale)
5. Using staffing agency (adds cost, margin, slow turnaround)

### Why They'd Sign Up for CertClip
- Can search portfolios by trade + region + specific code experience
- Can request a 30-minute live verification gig before committing to hire
- Reduces inspection risk; can document pre-hire due diligence

### Decision-Making Process
- Solo decision (owner) or owner + site supervisor
- Monthly subscription: needs owner approval; under $500/mo = easy yes if ROI is clear
- Will not sign a contract without a trial period or free tier

## ICP-2: The Mobile Journeyman (Primary Supply Side)

### Role
**Trade:** Electrician (journeyman level), Plumber, HVAC Tech, Welder  
**Status:** Active worker, 3–12 years experience, licensed at journeyman level or equivalent  
**Situation:** Either currently employed but open to better opportunities, OR in a job transition / relocation

### Profile Details
- **Age:** 26–42
- **Situation:** Working W-2 or 1099; has a valid license in 1–2 states; actively or passively job-seeking
- **Digital habits:** Uses smartphone daily; watches YouTube/TikTok; comfortable filming themselves
- **Income:** $55K–$90K/yr; wants more, or wants more stability

### Trade Focus (Priority Order)
1. Electricians (highest regulatory pressure, broadest job market)
2. Plumbers (similar dynamics, strong demand)
3. HVAC (EPA cert complexity adds value)
4. Welders (CWB/AWS certification gaps are common)

### Geographic Behavior
- Has worked in 2+ states/provinces in the last 5 years, OR
- Lives in a high-competition labor market (CA, TX, NY), OR
- Is actively relocating for work

### Urgency Trigger
- Currently between jobs (or just gave notice)
- Applying to multiple employers and getting ghosted / slow responses
- Recently moved and needs to re-establish reputation in a new market
- Tried to get a job above their current tier but lacked proof

### Current Workarounds
1. LinkedIn profile with cert photos (not verifiable)
2. Paper binder of certs handed to each employer
3. Union dispatch (works but limits opportunities to union shops)
4. Asking previous employers to be references (awkward, slow)
5. Personal website / portfolio (rare; tech-barrier for most)

### Why They'd Sign Up for CertClip
- Free to upload; instant portfolio shareable via link
- Verified badge makes them stand out in applications
- Portable across any employer, any state
- Paid assessment gig: earn income while getting certified

### Willingness to Pay
- Free tier: high conversion (low friction)
- $15–$25/month premium: yes if they see one faster hire as a result
- $49–$99 one-time assessment: yes if it's for a specific high-value job

## ICP-3: The Volume Recruiter (Secondary Demand Side — Staffing Agency)

### Role
**Title:** Branch Manager, Senior Recruiter, Compliance Coordinator  
**Company type:** Skilled trades staffing firm, regional or national  
**Placement volume:** 50–500 placements/year (trade-specific)

### Company Profile
- **Size:** $5M–$100M revenue
- **Geography:** Multi-state operation; priority markets TX, FL, CA, OH, IL
- **ATS:** Bullhorn, Hireology, or proprietary system
- **Pain point:** First-week send-back rate; cert documentation burden

### Urgency Trigger
- Just lost a client due to a compliance audit failure
- Opening a new branch in a new market and needs local talent verification
- Scaling up volume placements and recruiter time is becoming a bottleneck

### Current Workarounds
1. Manual cert collection via email/fax
2. Spreadsheet tracking of expiration dates
3. Outsourced background checks (don't cover skill quality)
4. Informal skill quizzes by phone/in-person

### Why They'd Sign Up for CertClip
- Pre-verified candidate pool reduces screening time per candidate by 60%+
- Credential wallet is audit-ready; client can download certification PDF
- ATS integration (planned v2) removes manual data entry
- "CertClip Verified" badge differentiates their candidate pool to clients

### Willingness to Pay
- $500–$2,000/month for team seat access + pre-verified pool
- ROI: 1 prevented send-back ($500–$2,000 in agency cost) = instant payback
