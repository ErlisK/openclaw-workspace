# CertClip — Partner Call Preparation Guide
**For: Exploratory calls with union/training center leadership**
Date: 2026-04-07

---

## Call Goal
Get verbal interest in being a "founding partner" — defined as:
- Willingness to introduce 2–3 retired/active journeymen as reviewer candidates
- 1–2 hours of Training Director time for code standards input
- Agreement to share the call outcome in writing (email confirmation)

**This is NOT a sales call.** It is a research and relationship-building call.

---

## Pre-Call Research Checklist
- [ ] Know the local's jurisdiction and current code standard
- [ ] Know their apprenticeship program length and specialty tracks
- [ ] Know their approximate membership size
- [ ] Review any recent news about their program (new facilities, partnerships)
- [ ] Have the landing page URL ready: certclip.com

---

## Call Script — 20 Minutes

### Opening (2 min)
"Thank you so much for taking the time. I'll be brief and honest: I'm an early-stage founder, I'm not selling anything yet, and I genuinely want your perspective before I build the wrong thing."

### Background (3 min)
"CertClip is a platform we're building to solve a specific problem I kept hearing about: journeymen electricians — or plumbers, pipefitters — who are extremely skilled but lose jobs or face weeks of delays because there's no good way to *show* that skill to a new employer. Paper certs don't prove quality. References take days. And when you cross a jurisdiction, you often have to re-prove everything from scratch."

### Discovery questions (10 min)
1. "In your experience, how do your graduates typically prove their skills when they go to a new employer after finishing the program?"
2. "What's the most common credential or competency gap you see when journeymen come to you from other programs or other states?"
3. "Have you had graduates who struggled to get hired even though you knew they were excellent workers? What was the obstacle?"
4. "How do your member contractors currently verify skill level before putting someone on a job site — especially on code-sensitive work?"
5. "Do you have senior instructors or retired journeymen who are doing any kind of mentoring or tutoring work on the side? Is that common?"

### Product brief (3 min)
"Based on what I'm hearing, let me describe what we're building and you tell me honestly if it makes sense:

Workers upload a 60-90 second video of themselves doing a specific task — conduit bending, a panel connection, whatever. A vetted journeyman in their trade watches it and leaves timestamped feedback, then issues a badge tied to that specific skill and the local code standard. Employers can search by trade, skill, and jurisdiction. It replaces the reference call and the paper cert binder with something verifiable and portable.

The thing I need most right now is 2-3 journeymen from your world who'd be willing to review a few videos and tell me if the process makes sense."

### Value prop for the org (2 min)
"For your organization, what we can offer is:
- Paid review gigs for your retired or senior journeymen — flexible, work from home, $25-40 per review
- You'd help us get the code taxonomy right for your jurisdiction
- Your program graduates would have a differentiator in the job market
- We'd co-brand the badges with your local's name if you want that"

### Close
"I'm not asking you to commit to anything today. I just want to know: does this sound like a real problem to you? And would you be willing to introduce me to one or two of your senior people so I can get their perspective?"

---

## Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "We already have our own credentialing system" | "That's exactly why I want to talk to you — what's working and what gaps do you still see? We want to complement, not compete, with what you've built." |
| "Our members might not be comfortable with video" | "Completely fair concern. That's one of the things I want to test — whether the video format works for tradespeople or if we need something different." |
| "How do you prevent the union from being disintermediated?" | "We don't replace the union card — we augment it. A CertClip badge says 'this person is union-trained AND here's proof of their specific skill in your jurisdiction.' It makes the union credential more visible, not less." |
| "What's in it for us?" | "Short term: paid work for your retired members, code taxonomy input, co-branded credentials for graduates. Long term: your local becomes the gold standard reviewer network in your region." |
| "I need to run this by our executive board" | "Totally understand. I'm not asking for a formal partnership yet — just an informal conversation with a couple of your journeymen. No commitment needed." |
