# The $50/Month Creator Repurposing Stack That Replaces a $3,000 Video Editor

Six months ago I was paying a video editor $1,500/month to cut clips from my podcast. She was good. The turnaround was 3–4 days per episode. I'd get 2–3 clips per episode if I was lucky, because manual editing doesn't scale.

I replaced that setup with a software stack that now costs me $47/month, turns around clips in under an hour, and generates 5–8 clips per episode. Here's exactly what I use.

## The Core Problem with Hiring Editors

Editing is a leverage trap. You hire an editor, which means:

1. You have to brief them on which moments you want (takes time)
2. They have to watch/listen to your full episode (more time)
3. They edit and send back revisions (time)
4. You review and request changes (more time)
5. Repeat

For a 60-minute episode, a good editor still needs 4–6 hours of billable work to produce 5 quality clips. At $50–75/hour, that's $200–450 per episode.

Even if you have that budget, the 3-day turnaround kills momentum. The best time to post a clip from Tuesday's episode is Wednesday or Thursday — not next Monday after revisions.

## The New Stack

### 1. ClipSpark ($9/month) — Clip Extraction + Captions
ClipSpark ingests your episode and automatically identifies the best moments using heuristic scoring (hook words, energy, questions, story markers, contrast phrases, specific numbers). 

What you get: 5–8 timestamped draft clips, burned-in captions, AI-generated titles, and direct publish to YouTube Shorts, LinkedIn, and TikTok.

Time investment: 10 minutes per episode to review and approve clips.

**The honest assessment:** The AI clip selection is about 70% as good as a trained editor's intuition. But it's instant, and you're reviewing 8 suggestions instead of watching the whole episode yourself. The miss rate is acceptable.

### 2. Descript ($24/month) — Script editing + studio-quality audio cleanup
If your recording has background noise, room echo, or filler words, Descript's AI cleans it. The "Studio Sound" feature alone is worth the subscription for podcasters with home studio setups.

For advanced trimming: if you want to cut a specific sentence from a clip, Descript lets you delete it from the transcript and it removes the audio. No timeline scrubbing.

I use ClipSpark for clip selection and initial captions, then sometimes run final clips through Descript for audio polish.

*Note: For most creators, ClipSpark's output is good enough to post directly. I only use Descript for episodes with audio quality issues.*

### 3. Canva Pro ($13/month) — Thumbnails + social graphics
Custom thumbnail templates for each platform. I have a saved set of Canva templates with my brand colors, fonts, and layouts. Takes 3 minutes to update the title text for each clip.

For YouTube Shorts, thumbnails are optional (Shorts often don't show thumbnails in the feed). But for LinkedIn and Instagram, a clean custom thumbnail increases click-through.

### 4. Buffer ($0/month free tier) — Scheduling
Queue clips for optimal posting times across platforms. Free tier covers 3 channels and 10 scheduled posts, which is enough for most podcasters.

**Total: $46/month.**

## The Weekly Workflow

**Tuesday (episode recording day):**
After recording, export the audio or video file. Upload to ClipSpark while you're eating lunch.

**Tuesday afternoon (15 minutes):**
ClipSpark has finished processing. Open the clips dashboard. Scan the 6–8 draft clips. Play each 30-second preview. Approve the 4–5 best ones. Click the AI-suggested title you like. Done.

**Tuesday evening (15 minutes):**
Open Buffer. Schedule the clips:
- Wednesday 9am: tactical clip (LinkedIn + YouTube Shorts)
- Thursday 6pm: story clip (TikTok + Instagram Reels)
- Friday 12pm: hot take clip (all platforms)

**Total active time: 30 minutes per episode.**

## What This Stack Doesn't Do

Being honest about the limitations:

**No motion graphics.** If you want animated title cards, lower thirds, or B-roll, you still need a human editor. ClipSpark + this stack is for talking-head/audio content, not polished documentary-style clips.

**No platform-specific creative optimization.** A skilled editor will know that a certain clip will perform better on LinkedIn vs. TikTok because of platform culture differences. The AI doesn't have that judgment.

**No brand voice consistency at scale.** If you're posting 20+ clips per week across multiple shows, you'll want editorial oversight to maintain consistency. This stack is optimized for solo creators doing one show.
