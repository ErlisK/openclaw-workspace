## What Makes a Good Shorts Clip from a Podcast

Not all podcast moments translate equally to Shorts. Here's what works and what doesn't.

### Works well

**The contrarian statement.**
"Everyone says you should post every day. I post 3 times a week and grew faster."
Sets up a conflict, forces the viewer to stay for the resolution.

**The specific number with a story.**
"I sent 200 cold emails in January. Here's exactly what happened."
Specificity creates credibility. The story creates forward motion.

**The direct question.**
"What would you do if your main income source disappeared tomorrow?"
Questions are psychologically hard to scroll past. The brain wants to answer.

**The process preview.**
"Here are the 4 things I check before posting any piece of content."
Lists create an implicit contract: "I'll stay for all 4."

### Doesn't work well

**Mid-thought clips.** If the clip starts in the middle of a sentence or the viewer needs context to understand what's happening, it'll underperform.

**Clips without captions.** 85% of Shorts are watched on mute. If your audio isn't readable, you've lost most of your audience before they hear a word.

**Clips that end abruptly.** The algorithm measures completion rate. A clip that ends naturally (thought resolved, story complete) will have better completion than one that cuts mid-sentence.

## The 3 Shorts That Come from Every Episode

After processing hundreds of podcast episodes through heuristic scoring, certain clip types appear reliably across all topics:

**Clip 1: The hot take.** Every good podcast has at least one moment where the host or guest says something most people disagree with or find surprising. This is your highest-scoring clip. Typical score: 0.82–0.94.

**Clip 2: The tactical moment.** The "here's how I actually do it" section. Specific process, real numbers, concrete steps. This clip performs best on YouTube specifically (educational intent). Typical score: 0.74–0.86.

**Clip 3: The story.** The narrative moment — a specific memory, a client story, a failure turned lesson. Stories are the most shareable format on any platform. Typical score: 0.70–0.82.

Every 60-minute episode has all three. ClipSpark finds them. You just approve.

## YouTube Shorts Optimization Checklist

Before uploading each clip, confirm:

- [ ] **Length:** 45–75 seconds (sweet spot for knowledge content)
- [ ] **Captions:** Present, readable on mobile (ClipSpark burns them in automatically)
- [ ] **Title:** Contains the key phrase someone might search (e.g., "how to grow a podcast")

- [ ] **Thumbnail:** Upload a custom thumbnail even for Shorts — it shows in search and channel browse
- [ ] **First 3 seconds:** Does the first sentence create a question or make a surprising claim?
