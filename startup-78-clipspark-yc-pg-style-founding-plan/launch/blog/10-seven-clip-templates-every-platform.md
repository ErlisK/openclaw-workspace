# 7 Clip Templates That Work on Every Platform (Copy and Use)

Most creators don't struggle with *making* clips. They struggle with *knowing what works*.

After analyzing thousands of short-form clips across TikTok, YouTube Shorts, LinkedIn, and Reels, there are 7 clip templates that consistently drive views, saves, and follows — regardless of niche.

These aren't vague formulas. They're specific structures with exact scripts you can fill in.

---

## Template 1: The Contrarian Hook

**Structure:** [Common belief] + "That's wrong." + [What's actually true]

**Why it works:** Pattern-interrupts the scroll. People stop when you challenge something they believe. The reveal creates a micro-curiosity gap.

**Script:**
> "[Common advice in your niche]. Here's why that's actually backwards.
> [Your counterintuitive take — explain in 30–45 seconds]
> The reason most people get this wrong is [root cause]."

**Example (podcasting niche):**
> "Everyone says you need great equipment to start a podcast. That's the worst advice I've heard.
> I grew a podcast to 10,000 downloads in 3 months on a $30 USB mic.
> The reason most people focus on equipment is it feels productive without the risk of actually putting your voice out there."

**Works best on:** TikTok, YouTube Shorts, LinkedIn  
**Optimal length:** 45–75 seconds

---

## Template 2: The Numbered Framework

**Structure:** "Here are [number] [things] that [outcome]"

**Why it works:** Sets a clear expectation (viewers know what they're getting). Drives completion because people want to see all N items. Creates saveability — people bookmark lists.

**Script:**
> "[Number] [things] I wish I knew before [relevant experience].
> Number one: [insight — 10 seconds]
> Number two: [insight — 10 seconds]
> Number three: [insight — 10 seconds]
> The one I use every week: number [X]."

**Example:**
> "3 things I wish I knew before starting a YouTube channel.
> Number 1: The algorithm doesn't care about subscribers — it cares about watch time per impression.
> Number 2: Your first 50 videos don't matter. They're practice.
> Number 3: The title matters 5x more than the thumbnail. Test it.
> The one I check every single week: number 1."

**Works best on:** YouTube Shorts, LinkedIn, Reels  
**Optimal length:** 60–90 seconds

---

## Template 3: The Before/After

**Structure:** "Before I knew this, I [bad outcome]. Now I [good outcome]. Here's what changed."

**Why it works:** Transformation is the most compelling story structure in short-form video. Viewers self-insert into the "before" state and want the "after."

**Script:**
> "Before I learned this, I [describe struggle/failure — 1–2 sentences].
> Now I [describe improvement — 1 sentence].
> The thing that changed everything: [explain the insight or system — 30–40 seconds]
> If you're stuck in the before state, this is the thing worth trying first."

**Works best on:** TikTok, Reels, YouTube Shorts  
**Optimal length:** 50–70 seconds

---

## Template 4: The Mistake Confession

**Structure:** "I made a [specific mistake]. Here's what it cost me and what I'd do differently."

**Why it works:** Vulnerability + specific stakes = emotional engagement. The "what I'd do differently" turns it educational. People share content that makes them feel less alone.

**Script:**
> "I made a [specific mistake] that cost me [specific cost — time, money, opportunities].
> Here's exactly what happened: [20-second story]
> What I'd do differently: [actionable alternative — 20 seconds]
> The thing is, [human insight that reframes the lesson as universal]"

**Works best on:** TikTok, LinkedIn, Reels  
**Optimal length:** 45–75 seconds

---

## Template 5: The Question Answer

**Structure:** [Real question from your audience] + Your direct answer

**Why it works:** Someone in your audience asked this — which means thousands more have the same question and haven't asked. When you say "a listener asked..." viewers self-identify. Answer their question and you've answered everyone's.

**Script:**
> "Someone [emailed me / DM'd me / asked in the comments]: [exact question]
> Here's what I told them:
> [Direct answer — 30–50 seconds]
> And if you're wondering the same thing, [personalize to direct viewer]"

**Works best on:** All platforms  
**Optimal length:** 45–75 seconds

---

## Template 6: The Step-by-Step

**Structure:** "Here's exactly how I [specific outcome] — step by step"

**Why it works:** Maximum practical value. Viewers watch to completion because each step builds on the last. High saveability because it's reference content.

**Script:**
> "Here's exactly how I [specific outcome] every [time period].
> Step 1: [concrete action — 10 seconds]
> Step 2: [concrete action — 10 seconds]
> Step 3: [concrete action — 10 seconds]
> The whole thing takes [time]. [Closing hook about why this matters]"

**Works best on:** YouTube Shorts, LinkedIn, TikTok (for tutorials)  
**Optimal length:** 60–90 seconds

---

## Template 7: The Observation

**Structure:** "[Something you noticed] + What it means for [audience]"

**Why it works:** Positions you as the expert who sees what others miss. Creates a "I should follow this person" reaction. Drives shares because people tag others who need to hear it.

**Script:**
> "I've been [doing X] for [timeframe]. Here's what I'm noticing:
> [2–3 specific observations — 30 seconds]
> What this means for [your audience type]:
> [Implication and recommendation — 20 seconds]
> I'll keep watching this. [Soft CTA to follow for updates]"

**Works best on:** LinkedIn, YouTube Shorts  
**Optimal length:** 50–75 seconds
