## The Old Way (Why It Doesn't Scale)

Traditional workflow for one clip:
- Scrub audio/video to find the moment: 20–45 minutes
- Cut in Descript, Adobe Premiere, or CapCut: 15–30 minutes
- Add captions manually: 10–20 minutes
- Resize for each platform: 10–15 minutes
- Write title and hashtags: 5–10 minutes
- Export and upload: 5 minutes

**Total: 65–120 minutes per clip.** For 5 clips from one episode: 5–10 hours.

No wonder most podcasters give up on short-form content.

## Anatomy of a Viral Podcast Clip

The clips that consistently blow up share a few traits:

**Hook in the first 3 seconds.** The algorithm decides in 3 seconds whether to keep showing your clip. Your opener must create a question in the viewer's mind: "Wait, what?" or "That can't be right."

Examples:
- "The number one mistake I made that cost me $200k was..." → stops the scroll
- "Nobody tells you this about email marketing, but..." → creates curiosity gap
- "I asked 500 founders what killed their startup. The answer surprised me." → narrative promise

**Tension and resolution.** The best clips have a mini-arc: setup, conflict, insight. Even 60 seconds is enough for a complete story.

**Captions, always.** 85% of social video is watched on mute. No captions = invisible content.

**Platform-native length:**
- TikTok/Reels/Shorts: 30–90 seconds performs best
- LinkedIn: 60–120 seconds
- X (Twitter): under 2 minutes
