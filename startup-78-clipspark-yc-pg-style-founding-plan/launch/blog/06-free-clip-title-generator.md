# Free Clip Title Generator: 47 Fill-in-the-Blank Templates for Short-Form Video

The title of your clip does two jobs at once:

1. It decides whether someone stops scrolling (hook)
2. It tells the platform's algorithm what your video is about (SEO)

Most creators spend 3 minutes picking a title, then wonder why their clips underperform. The title deserves more attention than the thumbnail.

Here are 47 fill-in-the-blank templates organized by hook type — plus a free AI tool to generate title options automatically.

---

## How to Use These Templates

Each template has a pattern, an explanation of why it works, and a fill-in example. Replace the brackets with your specific content.

The goal: a title that (1) stops the scroll and (2) contains the exact phrase your audience would search for.

---

## Curiosity Gap Titles (Best for discovery)

These titles create an open question the viewer's brain wants to resolve. They work because the human mind hates unfinished patterns.

**Template 1:** The [number] mistake [audience] make with [topic]
- Example: "The 3 mistakes podcasters make with their show descriptions"
- Why: Specific number creates credibility. "Mistake" implies a lesson. Audience-specific.

**Template 2:** Nobody talks about [specific thing]
- Example: "Nobody talks about the 4-hour post-recording crash"
- Why: Implies insider knowledge. Creates urgency to hear the thing nobody else says.

**Template 3:** I was wrong about [commonly held belief]
- Example: "I was wrong about batch recording — here's why"
- Why: Contradicts what the viewer already believes. Stops scroll.

**Template 4:** What happens when you [unusual action] for [time period]
- Example: "What happens when you post 3 Shorts per day for 30 days"
- Why: Experiential framing. Viewer lives the result vicariously.

**Template 5:** The [topic] advice that's actually hurting you
- Example: "The podcast growth advice that's actually hurting you"
- Why: Implies the viewer has been doing something wrong. High personal relevance.

**Template 6:** [Common belief] is [wrong/dead/a lie] — here's what actually works
- Example: "Cold DMs are dead — here's what actually works in 2025"
- Why: Contrarian + solution. Two hooks in one sentence.

**Template 7:** What [authority/expert type] don't tell you about [topic]
- Example: "What podcast coaches don't tell you about your first 100 episodes"
- Why: Implies a gap between official advice and reality.

---

## Numbered List Titles (Best for retention)

Lists create a psychological contract: "I'll tell you [number] things." The viewer stays to collect all [number].

**Template 8:** [Number] things I wish I knew before [experience]
- Example: "5 things I wish I knew before starting a weekly podcast"
- Why: Hindsight framing. High empathy with people about to start.

**Template 9:** [Number] [niche] tips that took me [time] to learn
- Example: "4 LinkedIn tips that took me 2 years to figure out"
- Why: Time investment implies genuine value. Condensed lessons = efficiency.

**Template 10:** [Number] reasons your [content type] isn't growing
- Example: "3 reasons your podcast isn't growing past 500 listeners"
- Why: Diagnostic. High personal relevance if viewer has this problem.

**Template 11:** [Number] [platform] hacks that [outcome]
- Example: "6 YouTube Shorts hacks that doubled my completion rate"
- Why: Platform-specific → good SEO. Outcome-quantified.

**Template 12:** [Number] tools every [audience] needs in [year]
- Example: "4 tools every solo podcaster needs in 2025"
- Why: Year-specific → signals freshness to algorithm.

**Template 13:** The [number] [platform/niche] rules I follow (that most people ignore)
- Example: "The 3 LinkedIn rules I follow that most people ignore"
- Why: Implies contrarian success formula.

---

## Story-Based Titles (Best for shares)

People share stories. These titles promise a narrative arc.

**Template 14:** How I [achievement] in [time] without [sacrifice]
- Example: "How I hit 1,000 podcast downloads without buying ads"
- Why: Achievement + constraint removed = aspiration without fear.

**Template 15:** From [starting point] to [result] in [time period]
- Example: "From 200 to 2,000 podcast subscribers in 90 days"
- Why: Transformation story. Classic narrative arc in one sentence.

**Template 16:** I [attempted something], here's what happened
- Example: "I posted 5 Shorts every day for a month — here's what happened"
- Why: Experiment framing. Suspense. Result withheld.

**Template 17:** The [role] who [did something unexpected] and [result]
- Example: "The solo founder who quit LinkedIn for 60 days and grew 300%"
- Why: Character + action + surprising outcome.

**Template 18:** What [X months/years] of [activity] taught me about [topic]
- Example: "What 3 years of weekly podcasting taught me about consistency"
- Why: Experience-based authority. High trust.

**Template 19:** I asked [number] [people type] about [question]. Here's what they said.
- Example: "I asked 50 podcasters what they'd do differently. Here's what they said."
- Why: Crowd-sourced wisdom. Implies diverse perspective.

---

## Contrarian / Hot Take Titles (Best for comments)

These titles say something most people disagree with. Comments → algorithm boost.

**Template 20:** Stop [commonly advised action]
- Example: "Stop posting every day — here's why"
- Why: Directly contradicts mainstream advice. Compelling even for disagreers.

**Template 21:** [Common tool/approach] is overrated. Use [alternative] instead.
- Example: "Descript is overrated for podcast clips. Use this instead."
- Why: Named brand creates controversy. Alternative creates curiosity.

**Template 22:** [Widely believed thing] doesn't work (and what does)
- Example: "Hashtags don't work on LinkedIn (and what does)"
- Why: Contrarian claim + solution = complete value in title.

**Template 23:** You're using [tool/platform] wrong
- Example: "You're using YouTube Shorts wrong"
- Why: Direct second-person address. Implies watcher is making a fixable mistake.

**Template 24:** [Popular number] is the wrong goal. Track [better metric] instead.
- Example: "Download numbers are the wrong goal. Track this instead."
- Why: Reframes how the viewer measures success.

---

## How-To Titles (Best for search)

These are the titles that rank in YouTube and Google search. Less viral, but evergreen traffic.

**Template 25:** How to [achieve outcome] in [time] (without [unwanted thing])
- Example: "How to grow a podcast audience in 90 days (without paid ads)"
- Why: Outcome + timeline + constraint removal.

**Template 26:** How to [do X] step by step (even if [objection])
- Example: "How to repurpose a podcast episode step by step (even if you hate editing)"
- Why: Step-by-step implies completeness. Even-if removes objection.

**Template 27:** The exact [process/script/template] I use to [achieve thing]
- Example: "The exact script I use to turn any podcast episode into 5 clips"
- Why: "Exact" implies precision and reproducibility.

**Template 28:** How to [do X] without [common tool/expense]
- Example: "How to caption short-form videos without paying for software"
- Why: Free/frugal angle. High search intent.

**Template 29:** [Platform] for beginners: [specific thing] explained
- Example: "YouTube Shorts for podcasters: the algorithm explained"
- Why: Beginner qualifier → narrows to high-intent audience.

**Template 30:** The beginner's guide to [topic] in [year]
- Example: "The beginner's guide to podcast repurposing in 2025"
- Why: SEO gold. Year signals freshness.

---

## Outcome-Promise Titles (Best for conversions)

These promise a specific result, making them high-converting but requiring delivery.

**Template 31:** [Do X] and [specific outcome] will follow
- Example: "Post 3 Shorts per week and these 5 things will happen to your channel"
- Why: Causal chain. Creates anticipation.

**Template 32:** The [adjective] way to [achieve outcome]
- Example: "The laziest way to get 10,000 podcast downloads"
- Why: "Laziest/fastest/cheapest" → removes friction as objection.

**Template 33:** [Outcome] in [time] — the [adjective] guide
- Example: "1,000 subscribers in 90 days — the no-budget guide"
- Why: Specific outcome + time + constraint.

**Template 34:** Want [outcome]? Do [specific action] first
- Example: "Want more podcast listeners? Fix your title strategy first"
- Why: Prescriptive. Implies the viewer is missing something actionable.

---

## Question Titles (Best for comments and algorithm)

Questions are psychologically hard to scroll past. The brain wants to answer.

**Template 35:** Are you making this [topic] mistake?
- Example: "Are you making this podcast SEO mistake?"
- Why: Second-person, diagnostic. Creates paranoia.

**Template 36:** What's your [thing]? (Here's mine)
- Example: "What's your podcast download goal? (Here's mine and how I set it)"
- Why: Invites participation. Community-building format.

**Template 37:** Could you [do X]? (Most people can't)
- Example: "Could you record a podcast episode in under 45 minutes? (Most people can't)"
- Why: Challenge framing. Creates urgency to prove yourself.

**Template 38:** What would you do if [hypothetical]?
- Example: "What would you do if your podcast host shut down tomorrow?"
- Why: Hypothetical stakes. Forces the viewer to think and engage.

---

## Specific Number + Claim Titles (Most credible)

Specific numbers signal real data, not vague claims. They stop scrolling and build trust.

**Template 39:** I analyzed [number] [pieces of content]. Here's what I found.
- Example: "I analyzed 500 podcast episodes. Here's what the top 10% have in common."
- Why: Research-backed authority. Number implies rigor.

**Template 40:** [X]% of [group] are [doing something wrong]. Are you one of them?
- Example: "73% of podcasters make this one title mistake. Are you one of them?"
- Why: Statistic creates authority. Question creates personal relevance.

**Template 41:** [Number] [things] I did to go from [X] to [Y]
- Example: "7 things I did to go from 300 to 3,000 podcast downloads in 6 months"
- Why: Specific list + before/after transformation.

**Template 42:** [Specific number] [metric] in [specific time] — here's how
- Example: "10,000 YouTube Shorts views in 30 days — here's how"
- Why: Goal-state with proof of concept.

---

## Emotional/Identity Titles (Best for shares among specific communities)

These titles make the viewer say "this is me" and share with others like them.

**Template 43:** The [audience] guide to [topic]
- Example: "The solo podcaster's guide to building a loyal audience"
- Why: Identity-specific. People share things that represent them to others like them.

**Template 44:** If you're a [audience member], you need to [action]
- Example: "If you're a podcaster under 1,000 downloads, you need to read this"
- Why: Narrow identity qualifier = higher relevance for the right viewer.

**Template 45:** What nobody tells you when you start [activity]
- Example: "What nobody tells you when you start podcasting seriously"
- Why: Implies community knowledge withheld from newcomers.

**Template 46:** For every [creator type] who's been told [bad advice]
- Example: "For every podcaster who's been told 'just stay consistent'"
- Why: Empathy framing. Validates a common frustration.

**Template 47:** I'm a [type of creator] and I've been [doing something] for [X years]. Here's the truth.
- Example: "I'm a solo podcaster and I've been posting weekly for 3 years. Here's the truth."
- Why: Authority + vulnerability + promise of honesty.
